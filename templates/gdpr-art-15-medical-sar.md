# GDPR Article 15 Subject Access Request — Medical / Healthcare File

A formal request for every record the GP / paediatrician / hospital holds on your child. Same free / 30-day / no-question-asked basis as the school SAR. Available to any parent with parental responsibility.

Particularly valuable for revealing: missed appointments (with who-brought-the-child entries), the alienating parent's account of medical concerns (often different from what they later tell the court), and any clinician's notes recording the child's expressed wishes about contact.

## Template letter

> [Your full name]
> [Your address]
> [Date]
>
> The Data Protection Officer
> [GP practice / hospital name + address]
>
> **Subject Access Request — [child's full name], DOB [date]**
>
> Dear Data Protection Officer,
>
> I am writing to make a Subject Access Request under Article 15 of the [EU GDPR / UK GDPR] and the Data Protection Act 2018 for personal data concerning my child, [child's full name], date of birth [DOB], a patient at [practice / hospital].
>
> I am the child's [father / mother] and hold parental responsibility. A copy of [the birth certificate / court order] is enclosed.
>
> Please provide a copy of the complete medical record, including:
>
> 1. All consultation notes and clinician's narrative entries.
> 2. All appointment-attendance records, including: date scheduled, date attended (or DNA), and who brought the child.
> 3. All referrals made and received.
> 4. All correspondence sent or received about my child (including from social care, school, or other healthcare providers).
> 5. Any safeguarding flags or notes.
> 6. Any records of my child's expressed views regarding family contact, if recorded.
> 7. Information about any third parties to whom records have been disclosed.
>
> I understand that under Article 15, the response is due within one calendar month, without charge.
>
> Please contact me at [email] / [phone] if any clarification is required.
>
> Yours sincerely,
>
> [Signature]
> [Printed name]
>
> *Enclosures: [copy of birth certificate / court order]*

## After you receive the response

- Cross-reference appointment-attendance records to your [contact log](./contact-log-spreadsheet.md) — gaps where the other parent claimed the child was with them but the medical record shows missed appointments are dispositive.
- Note any clinician entries recording the child's stated views — these are admissible third-party records.
- Save the complete pack for any future expert engagement.
