# Evidence — International Institutional Positions on Parental Alienation

A cross-jurisdictional thematic summary of institutional positions taken by professional regulators, learned societies, courts and women's-rights bodies on the parental-alienation construct. Compiled from the AntiAlienate knowledge base v2 corpus (35+ jurisdictions, 30+ case studies). CC BY 4.0.

## Apex-court positions

### Recognition pole (apex-court recognises PA as substantive frame)
- **India** — Lt Col Vivek Singh v Romani Singh, (2017) 3 SCC 231 (Supreme Court of India, Chelameswar J. & Sikri J., 13 February 2017). Apex-court decision that expressly defines "The Parental Alienation Syndrome" on the record. The only Indian Supreme Court decision engaging PAS terminology directly. Subsequent Delhi HC line (ABC v XYZ 2023 SCC OnLine Del 6099; A v B 2026 SCC OnLine Del 276) crystallises PA as 'extreme mental cruelty' under HMA s.13(1)(ia). Cross-link: case-study:vivek-singh-v-romani-singh-2017-india.
- **Singapore** — TEN v TEO and another appeal [2020] SGHCF 20 (Debbie Ong J, then Presiding Judge FJC). Two-limb doctrine: court cannot force children to love a parent but expects custodial parent to cease alienating behaviour and support reunification. s.68 Women's Charter maintenance duty independent of access. Cross-link: case-study:ten-v-teo-2020-sghcf-20-singapore.
- **Hong Kong** — H v W [2021] HKCA 733 (Lam VP, Yuen JA, B Chu J, 24 May 2021). Engages PA as separately pleaded ground of appeal via SIR + SJE framework without endorsing PAS as clinical construct. Cross-link: case-study:h-v-w-2021-hkca-733-hong-kong.
- **South Africa** — T.L.D v B.G [2023] ZAGPJHC 801 (Adams J, Gauteng Local Division Johannesburg, 13 July 2023). Leading SA High Court authority crystallising PA as clinically and judicially recognised phenomenon under Children's Act s.7(1)(c) attitude-of-parents factor. Cross-link: case-study:tld-v-bg-2023-zagpjhc-801-south-africa.
- **Scotland** — NF v AF [2025] CSOH 13 (Lord Stuart, Court of Session Outer House, 5 February 2025). Strongest single Scottish judicial PA finding to date — characterised knowingly false allegations as direct emotional abuse of the child, decided on welfare/abuse grounds rather than contested 'PAS' label. Cross-link: case-study:nf-v-af-2025-csoh-13-scotland.
- **Poland** — Sąd Najwyższy, uchwała III CZP 20/25 z 3 października 2025 r. Pro-enforcement apex stance restoring effectiveness of art. 598¹⁶ § 1 k.p.c. monetary sanctions against alienating parent, without engaging clinical PAS diagnosis. First CEE apex in 2017-2025 EU sequence. Cross-link: case-study:sn-iii-czp-20-25-poland-2025.
- **England & Wales** — Re Y [2026] EWFC 38 (Sir Andrew McFarlane P, 2026). UK-wide evaluator-quality apex authority — HCPC-registration requirement for PA evaluators (flows UK-wide via Health and Social Care Act 2008 HCPC statutory framework). Cross-link: case-study:re-y-2026-ewfc-38.

### Middle pole (apex-court adopts proportionalist or differential-diagnosis frame)
- **Mexico** — SCJN Acción de Inconstitucionalidad 11/2016 (Oaxaca, ponente Ministra Norma Lucía Piña Hernández, octubre 2017). Doctrina: figura sí, automatismo no. Validates legislative recognition of AP as juridically relevant phenomenon but invalidates automatic sanction (suspension/loss of patria potestad). Subsequently extended in AI 74/2024 (Tlaxcala, 29 abril 2025). Cross-link: case-study:scjn-ai-11-2016-oaxaca-mexico.
- **Spain** — Tribunal Supremo, Sala Primera, STS 519/2017 de 22 de septiembre de 2017. Confirms custodia compartida supporting itself on parental-manipulation finding without endorsing SAP syndrome. Disposición adicional 17ª LOPIVI 2021 — only statutory PA-inadmission law globally (alongside Brazil Lei 12.318/2010 statutory recognition — the two world statutory PA jurisdictions point in opposite directions). Cross-link: case-study:sts-519-2017-spain.

### Critique pole (apex-court disqualifies PAS construct)
- **Italy** — Cassazione Civile Sez. I, ordinanze 13217/2021 (17 maggio 2021) → 9691/2022 (24 marzo 2022) → 4595/2025. Strongest EU apex critique line: PAS is "pseudo-scientific" and cannot found measures depriving parental relations. Combined with Riforma Cartabia (D.lgs. 149/2022) restricting CTU PAS-based assessments. Cross-link: case-study:cassazione-13217-2021-italy + case-study:cassazione-9691-2022-italy.
- **Germany** — BVerfG 17.11.2023 – 1 BvR 1076/23 (Bundesverfassungsgericht). Constitutional Court qualifies SAP as "outdated and scientifically refuted concept"; PAS-based Sachverständigengutachten constitutionally untenable. Cross-link: case-study:bverfg-1-bvr-1076-23-germany-2023.
- **Colombia** — Corte Constitucional T-526/2023 (M.P. Diana Fajardo Rivera, Sala Tercera, 30 noviembre 2023). Proscribes SAP as diagnostic instrument citing 5 convergent state authorities (COLPSIC + ICBF + Procuraduría + MinSalud + constitutional analysis). Widest LATAM institutional critique coalition. Subsequently extended in T-226/2024.

## Strasbourg / supranational layer

### European Court of Human Rights — Article 8 procedural-due-diligence standard
- **Lombardo v Italy** (App no 25704/11, 29 January 2013) — foundational Strasbourg PA-adjacent decision; Italian authorities failed to enforce contact rights. Cross-link: case-study:lombardo-v-italy-echr-25704-11-2013.
- **Strumia v Italy** (App no 53377/13, 23 June 2016) — Italian authorities failed to safeguard father-child relationship after mother's alienating behaviour. Cross-link: case-study:strumia-v-italy-echr-53377-13-2016.
- **Improta v Italy** (App no 66396/14, 4 May 2017) — Italian authorities' failure to enforce contact and address alienating behaviour by primary carer. Cross-link: case-study:improta-v-italy-echr-66396-14-2017.

Triptych establishes Article 8 procedural-due-diligence positive obligation: member states must take 'adequate and effective measures' to enable parent-child contact restoration. Subsequently engaged in Cassazione 9691/2022 (Italy).

### UN / Inter-American
- **UN Special Rapporteur on Violence against Women Reem Alsalem — Report A/HRC/53/36 (12 August 2022)** — addresses PA construct as tactic against DV-survivor mothers. Subsequently cited internationally as critique-anchor (Corte Constitucional Colombia T-526/2023; IEFH/IGVM Belgium Recommandation 2023/001; multiple LATAM5 institutional anchors).
- **MESECVI Declaration 13 April 2023** — Inter-American mechanism on PA tactical deployment in family courts.
- **CEDAW 2016 Informe Sombra Argentina** (FEIM / Bianco + Correa) — names 'falso Síndrome de Alienación Parental' as discriminatory operator-judicial tactic in CSA cases.

## Professional-regulator / learned-society positions

### Critique-camp (formal published position against)
- **Italy** — CNOP (Consiglio Nazionale Ordine degli Psicologi) institutional commentary on Cassazione 13217/2021 + 9691/2022 line.
- **Brazil** — Conselho Federal de Psicologia (CFP) Nota Técnica 4/2022 + CFESS. Brazil's two professional regulators issued formal positions while statute (Lei 12.318/2010) codifies AP — institutional critique pole roughly balances clinical recognition pole.
- **Argentina** — FePRA Pronunciamiento 14.12.2019 + 7 provincial Colegios professional positions against SAP/AP as diagnostic category.
- **Colombia** — COLPSIC Posición Gremial 16.11.2023 (deliberate pivot from 2017 recognition-leaning Concepto Técnico). Distinguishes 'SAP' (rejected) from 'alienación parental' (describable relational phenomenon).
- **Belgium** — IEFH/IGVM Recommandation 2023/001 — STRUCTURALLY UNIQUE EU6 critique anchor at federal gender-equality regulator (NL+FR bilingual) — explicitly: "le syndrome d'aliénation parentale ne peut être utilisé pour minimiser la violence entre (ex-)partenaires."
- **France** — CIIVISE Durand 2024 institutional engagement; AFFEC + Atelier des Droits Sociaux + Choisir la Cause des Femmes academic-feminist critique cluster.
- **Spain** — STS 519/2017 + LOPIVI 2021 Disp. adicional 17ª inadmission language; Defensor del Pueblo + Consejo General del Poder Judicial 2020 sectoral position.

### Silent or middle (no formal published position)
- **USA** — APA Resolution on Male Violence (1996) + APA 2019 Resolution on Maltreatment of Children + AACAP Practice Parameter 2011: recognise relational distress + child-maltreatment frameworks but DO NOT endorse PA as stand-alone diagnostic entity. DSM-5-TR retains CAPRD V995.51 / Z62.898 as relational V/Z-code (not diagnosis-of-child).
- **United Kingdom** — Royal College of Psychiatrists + BPS: ICD-11 caregiver-child relationship problem (QE52.0) coding available without committing to PA position. HCPC: no PA-specific code-of-conduct guidance but Re Y [2026] EWFC 38 HCPC-registration kitemark applies UK-wide.
- **Netherlands** — NIP + NVvP institutionally silent. Positional weight in three documents (NJI Richtlijn 2020 + Expertteam 2021 + Kinderombudsman KOM003/2014) — not in associations.
- **Germany** — DJI 2023 institutional review + BÄK (Bundesärztekammer) periodic positions; no apex regulator endorsement of PAS construct.
- **Australia** — AHPRA + Psychology Board of Australia silent. AFCC Model Standards 2006/2022 (international) function as de facto practice standard.
- **Canada** — CPA + provincial Colleges silent. NAWL 2024 FEWO brief calls for statutory ban on PA accusations — unique critique-camp policy infrastructure with no clear US parallel.
- **New Zealand** — NZ Psychologists Board silent at PA-specific level. 2018 Joint Practice Note + Leonetti UoA UCLA Pacific Basin 2023 academic-critique anchor.
- **India** — IPS + IAPP institutionally silent. NIMHANS Bengaluru recognition-cluster + 4 women's-rights-org institutional critique register (Lawyers Collective WRI + Majlis + MASUM + Sakshi) PWDVA/CEDAW frame.
- **Mexico** — Sociedad Mexicana de Psicología (SMP) institutionally silent — anomalous within LATAM5 (vs CFP BR + FePRA AR + COLPSIC CO).
- **Chile** — CdPCh institutionally silent + Defensoría de la Niñez silent (double silence convergent). Rechazo institucional vía Cámara de Diputadas + Ejecutivo MMEG + ANMM, NOT vía colegio profesional psicología.
- **South Africa** — HPCSA Professional Board for Psychology + SACSSP silent. SA Standards of Practice for Forensic Assessments 2021 (Martalas-chair) discipline-level evaluator-quality anchor.

## Structural patterns across the corpus

1. **APEX-COURT POSITION ≠ REGULATOR POSITION**: India apex-recognition + IPS silence; Mexico apex-middle + SMP silence; Chile no-apex-PA + CdPCh silence. Apex-court engagement and regulator engagement are loosely coupled.

2. **EU6 CRITIQUE-DOMINANT, ASIA RECOGNITION-DOMINANT, LATAM5 CRITIQUE-DOMINANT**: Major regional asymmetries. EU6 (IT + DE + ES + FR + NL + BE) all critique-pole. Asian apex cluster (IN + SG + HK) recognition-pole. LATAM5 (AR + BR + MX + CO + CL) overall critique-dominant (Brazil sole statutory-recognition outlier).

3. **THREE STATUTORY PA JURISDICTIONS WORLD: BRAZIL + SPAIN + JAPAN**: Brazil (Lei 12.318/2010 codifies AP positively); Spain (LOPIVI 2021 Disp. ad. 17ª inadmits SAP nominally); Japan (Act No. 33 of 2024 1.4.2026 effective joint-custody reform addressing 子の連れ去り / 引き離し — directionally opposite Australia 2023 s.61DA repeal).

4. **INSTITUTIONAL CRITIQUE REGISTER IS WOMEN'S-RIGHTS-ORG-LED IN GLOBAL SOUTH**: India (Lawyers Collective WRI + Majlis + MASUM + Sakshi) + Africa (Mosaic SA + FIDA Kenya + Project Alert/WACOL Nigeria + FIDA-Ghana + TAWLA Tanzania + NCCM Egypt). Distinct from German clinician-led critique cluster (Zimmermann/Walper/Fichtner) or Spanish Vaccaro coinage.

5. **MULTI-TIER INTERNATIONAL CRITIQUE-ANCHOR CONVERGENCE**: Strasbourg Art 8 procedural-due-diligence + UN Alsalem A/HRC/53/36 (2022) + MESECVI Belém do Pará (2023) + LATAM5 domestic-apex (Colombia T-526/23; Belgium IEFH 2023/001) operate in convergent multi-tier configuration.
