# Evidence — Evaluator-Quality Regulation Across Jurisdictions

A structural synthesis of evaluator-quality regulatory mechanisms governing expert assessments in PA-adjacent family-court proceedings across jurisdictions, compiled from the AntiAlienate knowledge base v2 corpus. The pattern is recent (post-2018) and operates through diverse routes: apex case-law (E&W Re Y), statute-and-rules (Australia 2023 Sch 2; Italy Riforma Cartabia), professional standards (SA 2021), academic-legal critique (NZ Leonetti), and institutional advisory (NL Expertteam 2021). ## The evaluator-quality reform wave

Between 2018 and 2026, multiple jurisdictions independently developed regulatory mechanisms to address evaluator-quality concerns in PA-adjacent expert assessment. The pattern is structurally significant: similar concerns about under-credentialed evaluators producing influential family-court reports surfaced across common-law and civil-law jurisdictions, with each developing distinct regulatory routes.

### 1. England and Wales — Re Y [2026] EWFC 38 (apex case-law route)
**Sir Andrew McFarlane P, 20 February 2026.** UK-wide evaluator-quality apex authority. McFarlane P held that 'permission should not be given for the instruction of an expert psychologist who is neither registered by a relevant statutory body, nor chartered by the BPS.' HCPC-registration kitemark for PA evaluators flows UK-wide via HCPC statutory framework under Health and Social Care Act 2008. Operationalises against the principal exemplar — Melanie Gill (excluded UK practitioner; not HCPC-registered; not BPS-chartered). Re Y findings set aside on the basis of her assessment.

The English Re-arc (Re A 2019 → Re S 2020 → Re H-N 2021 → Re C 2023 → Re Y 2026) constitutes the principal apex case-law evaluator-quality development. Cross-link: case-study:re-y-2026-ewfc-38 + jurisdiction:england-and-wales.

### 2. Australia — Family Law Amendment Act 2023 Schedule 2 (statute-and-rules route)
**In force 6 May 2024.** Schedule 2 of the 2023 Amendment Act replaces the prior 'family report' label with the tighter Court Children's Report (CCR) standard, formalises the FCFCOA Family Report Writers Network (FRW pool) as the institutional vehicle, and operationalises evaluator-quality standards parallel in policy intent to Re Y EWFC 38 but operationalised differently.

AHPRA / Psychology Board of Australia statutory registration is the load-bearing credential for any court-facing PA work. Practitioners listed without AHPRA-psychology registration (PACFA, ACA, AASW, FDRP) are flagged with regulatory-analogue caveat. Operational distinction: FLA s.121 anonymisation regime prevents named-evaluator case-law equivalent to Re Y; evaluator-quality concerns are addressed via Schedule 2 reform rather than named-evaluator appellate decision. Cross-link: case-study:green-and-green-2024-fedcfamc1f-896 + jurisdiction:australia.

### 3. South Africa — Standards of Practice for Forensic Assessments 2021 (professional-standards route)
**Martalas-chaired task force document, 2021.** Discipline-level evaluator-quality anchor. HPCSA 4-category psychology registration (clinical / counselling / educational / forensic) is the load-bearing credential. The 2021 Standards document operationalises evaluator-quality standards through professional-association consensus rather than apex case-law or statute.

SA structural specificity: T.L.D v B.G [2023] ZAGPJHC 801 (Adams J) operates at substantive PA-recognition level without expressly addressing evaluator-quality; the Martalas 2021 Standards address evaluator-quality at the professional-standards level. The two operate in parallel rather than in integration. Cross-link: case-study:tld-v-bg-2023-zagpjhc-801-south-africa + jurisdiction:south-africa.

### 4. New Zealand — Leonetti UCLA Pacific Basin 2023 (academic-legal critique route)
**Carrie Leonetti (Associate Professor, University of Auckland Law School), 2023.** 'Combatting a Dangerous American Export: The Need for Professional Regulation of Psychologists in the New Zealand Family Court' (UCLA Pacific Basin Law Journal) is the load-bearing critique-camp academic anchor calling for stronger NZ Psychologists Board regulation of psychologists who deploy the PA construct in Family Court.

NZ structural specificity: 2018 Joint Practice Note for Specialist Report Writers (Principal Family Court Judge + Ministry of Justice + NZLS Family Law Section + NZPB + NZPsS + NZCCP multi-party signature) is the multi-institutional regulatory anchor. Leonetti's UCLA 2023 + Oxford IJLPF 2025 articles argue for stronger NZPB engagement. Standards-of-evidence concerns sit at Practice Note + NZPB regulatory layer rather than apex case-law. Cross-link: jurisdiction:new-zealand.

### 5. Italy — Riforma Cartabia D.lgs. 149/2022 (statute route)
**In force 2023.** Restricts CTU (Consulenza Tecnica d'Ufficio) evaluations based on PAS construct. Operationalises the Cassazione critique line (13217/2021 + 9691/2022 + 4595/2025) at statutory-procedural level. Italy operates the EU's most integrated apex case-law + statute + Strasbourg multi-layer evaluator-quality architecture.

Italian structural specificity: Riforma Cartabia + Cassazione triad operate together to address evaluator-quality at construct-validity level (disqualifying PAS-grounded CTU). Distinct from E&W Re Y which addresses evaluator-quality at credentialing level (HCPC-registration requirement) without disqualifying PAS as construct. Cross-link: case-study:cassazione-9691-2022-italy + jurisdiction:italy.

### 6. Netherlands — Expertteam Ouderverstoting 2021 (institutional advisory route)
**Adviesrapport januari 2021** (chair prof. dr. Catrin Finkenauer, UU) recommends RvdK feitenonderzoek strengthening + Methode Scheidings Advies Team (SAT) introduction. Methodological middle-stance: erkenning van het fenomeen mét voorbehouden. Operates institutional-advisory route rather than apex case-law / statute / professional-standards routes.

NL structural specificity: NL operates the three-document positional architecture (Expertteam 2021 + NJI Richtlijn 2020 + Kinderombudsman KOM003/2014). Evaluator-quality concerns addressed at institutional-advisory level rather than apex case-law. Cross-link: case-study:expertteam-ouderverstoting-2021-netherlands + jurisdiction:netherlands.

### 7. Singapore — Family Justice Act 2014 + CAPS (institutional integration route)
**FJA 2014 + Counselling and Psychological Service (CAPS).** Family Justice Courts integrate counselling-and-psychological-service into the family-court process. Court-directed family conferences and reunification-support sessions are administratively embedded in FJC. CAPS team integration is the institutional analogue to E&W Cafcass + Australian FRW pool + Canadian OCL + SA Family Advocate.

Singapore structural specificity: TEN v TEO [2020] SGHCF 20 (Debbie Ong J) operates substantive PA-recognition without separately addressing evaluator-quality — institutional integration approach addresses evaluator-quality through court-attached service architecture rather than independent professional credentialing. Cross-link: jurisdiction:singapore.

### 8. Hong Kong — SIR/SJE dual-track + LRC HK 2005 Parental Responsibility Model (institutional + proposed-reform route)
**Social Investigation Report (SIR) + Single Joint Expert (SJE).** SWD social welfare officer prepares SIR; SJE clinical-psychology evidence joint-instruction. The SIR/SJE dual track is the HK institutional analogue to Singapore CAPS / England Cafcass / Australia FRW pool. Children Proceedings (Parental Responsibility) Bill (first floated 2015, revisited 2024 LegCo, NOT YET ENACTED) would introduce LRC HK 2005 Saunders Report Parental Responsibility Model proposing further reform.

HK structural specificity: H v W [2021] HKCA 733 (Lam VP, Yuen JA, B Chu J) engaged SIR + SJE framework substantively without separately addressing evaluator-quality. HKPS voluntary register (NO statutory psychology scheme) is structurally weaker than UK HCPC or Australia AHPRA — recognised institutional gap pending Bill enactment. Cross-link: jurisdiction:hong-kong.

## Structural observations

### 1. Diverse routes to similar concerns
Across 8 jurisdictions surveyed, evaluator-quality concerns surface independently but are addressed through **structurally diverse routes**: apex case-law (E&W) + statute-and-rules (Australia + Italy) + professional standards (SA) + academic-legal critique (NZ) + institutional advisory (NL) + institutional integration (Singapore + HK). The route taken depends on the jurisdiction's regulatory architecture maturity and judicial-output capacity.

### 2. Re Y EWFC 38 is the strongest apex case-law route
**England and Wales Re Y [2026] EWFC 38** is the strongest single apex case-law evaluator-quality decision in the corpus. McFarlane P's HCPC-registration kitemark requirement flows UK-wide via HCPC statutory framework. The named-evaluator (Melanie Gill) approach is operationally significant — directly disciplines individual practice through Court of Appeal precedent.

### 3. FLA s.121 anonymisation precludes named-evaluator route in Australia
**Australian operational distinction**: FLA s.121 anonymisation regime prevents named-evaluator case-law equivalent to Re Y. Schedule 2 reform (2023 Amendment Act, in force 6.5.2024) is the structural alternative — addressing evaluator-quality through statutory CCR regime rather than appellate-named-evaluator decisions.

### 4. Re Y's UK-wide reach via HCPC statutory framework
**Cross-constituent UK reach**: Re Y formally an E&W decision but HCPC-registration requirement applies UK-wide via Health and Social Care Act 2008 HCPC statutory framework (Scotland + Northern Ireland + Wales constituent jurisdictions). Distinguishes Re Y from purely-jurisdictional apex decisions.

### 5. Statutory PA inadmission (Spain LOPIVI) does NOT address evaluator-quality directly
Spanish LOPIVI 2021 Disposición adicional 17ª inadmits SAP-label use in family-court reasoning but does NOT directly address evaluator-quality regulation. Spanish evaluator-quality is addressed through COP psicología provincial regulation rather than statute. Distinguishes Spain from Italy where Riforma Cartabia statutorily restricts PAS-grounded CTU evaluations.

### 6. Common-law jurisdictions favor named-evaluator + institutional-integration routes
Common-law jurisdictions (E&W + Australia + SA + NZ + Singapore + HK) favor named-evaluator (E&W Re Y) + institutional-integration (Singapore CAPS / Australia FRW / SA Family Advocate / HK SIR-SJE) routes. Civil-law jurisdictions (Italy + Netherlands) favor statutory-procedural (Italy Riforma Cartabia) + institutional-advisory (NL Expertteam 2021) routes.

### 7. Regulatory architecture maturity correlates
Newer statutory psychology professions tend to operate at institutional-integration level rather than independent-regulator level:
- **Japan Kōnin Shinrishi** (statutory only since 2017): operates via institutional integration.
- **Ghana Psychology Council** (statutory since 2013): operates via institutional integration.
- **Hong Kong HKPS** (voluntary only, no statutory): operates via SIR+SJE institutional dual-track.

Mature statutory regulators (UK HCPC + AU AHPRA + SA HPCSA + US state boards) support named-evaluator + statute-and-rules approaches.

### 8. The Melanie Gill / Randy Rand parallel
Cross-jurisdictionally, evaluator-quality concerns operationalise around specific named exclusion exemplars:
- **UK / E&W** — Melanie Gill (not HCPC-registered, not BPS-chartered, Re Y findings set aside).
- **USA / California** — Randy Rand EdD (CA Board of Psychology license inactive since 2009 following disciplinary action; Family Bridges programme operates as 'educational workshop' outside CABOP oversight).
- **Germany** — Reinhart Wolff (excluded for safeguarding reasons).

The exemplar-disclosure pattern is operationally significant — named regulatory exclusions function as evaluator-quality enforcement triggers, distinct from anonymised reform routes (Australia Schedule 2; Italian Riforma Cartabia).
