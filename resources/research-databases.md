# Research Databases — Where the Evidence Lives

The Highway's pointer list for primary-source PA research and case law. Free / low-cost / open where possible. Last reviewed 2026-05.

---

## Academic & Clinical Research

### PubMed (NIH/NLM) — free
- URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
- Coverage: 35M+ biomedical & clinical citations
- PA-relevant queries: `"parental alienation"`, `"alienating behaviors"`, `"resist-refuse dynamics"`
- API: E-utilities (eutils.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) — no auth, generous rate limits
- Best for: psychiatry, child psychology, custody-evaluation methodology

### CrossRef — free
- URL: https://search.crossref.org/
- Coverage: 130M+ DOIs across publishers
- API: https://api.crossref.org/ — no auth
- Best for: catching PA papers PubMed misses (law journals, social work, family-studies)

### Google Scholar — free (browser only)
- URL: https://scholar.google.com/
- Use the "Cited by" graph to map influence forward from foundational papers (Gardner 1985, Warshak 2001, Bernet 2010)
- Set up alerts on `"parental alienation"` for weekly digests
- Limitation: no API; respect rate limits

### SSRN (Social Science Research Network) — free
- URL: https://www.ssrn.com/index.cfm/en/lps/family-juvenile-law/
- Family & Juvenile Law eJournal: working papers and pre-prints from family-law academics
- Best for: catching legal-academic arguments before they hit law-review print

### PsycNet (APA) — paywalled, abstracts free
- URL: https://psycnet.apa.org/
- Gold standard for clinical psychology citations. Most law libraries provide access.

## US Case Law

### CourtListener (Free Law Project) — free, generous API
- URL: https://www.courtlistener.com/
- Coverage: federal + state appellate opinions, oral arguments, financial-disclosure data
- API: https://www.courtlistener.com/api/rest/v4/ — Basic Auth or token
- Best for: full-text search of "parental alienation" across US opinions
- Bulk: https://www.courtlistener.com/api/bulk-info/ — opinion JSONs by court

### Google Scholar Case Law — free
- URL: https://scholar.google.com/ (select "Case law")
- Coverage: US federal + state, with cite-by graph
- Best for: quick lookup; build forward-citation maps

### Justia — free
- URL: https://law.justia.com/cases/
- US Supreme Court + state supreme + federal appellate
- Best for: clean reading view of full opinions

### Caselaw Access Project (Harvard) — free
- URL: https://case.law/
- 6.4M cases, 1658–2018, full text & structured metadata
- Best for: historical research, doctrinal evolution

## UK / Ireland Case Law

### BAILII — free
- URL: https://www.bailii.org/
- Coverage: UK + Ireland court decisions, Family Court (EWFC), Court of Appeal (EWCA Civ), High Court (EWHC)
- Recent decisions feed: https://www.bailii.org/recent-decisions.html
- Best for: family-court rulings, especially Re-named anonymised cases

### Family Law Week — paywalled summaries free
- URL: https://www.familylawweek.co.uk/
- Curated case digests with practitioner commentary
- Best for: practitioner perspective on emerging trends

### Transparency Project — free
- URL: https://www.transparencyproject.org.uk/
- Family-court reform commentary + reported judgments
- Best for: public-interest analysis of contentious rulings

## EU / International

### HUDOC (European Court of Human Rights) — free
- URL: https://hudoc.echr.coe.int/
- Article 8 (right to family life) jurisprudence
- Best for: regional cross-border PA arguments

### Council of Europe — free
- URL: https://www.coe.int/en/web/children/
- Children's rights instruments and committee reports

### CURIA (Court of Justice of the EU) — free
- URL: https://curia.europa.eu/
- Brussels IIa / IIb regulation interpretations on jurisdiction in custody disputes

## Practical Tools

### Zotero — free, open source
- URL: https://www.zotero.org/
- Reference manager with one-click capture from any database above
- Build a shared group library: "Parental Alienation — Core Sources"

### Connected Papers — free tier
- URL: https://www.connectedpapers.com/
- Visual citation graph from any seed paper. Start with Bernet 2010 or Warshak 2015.

### Litmaps — free tier
- URL: https://www.litmaps.com/
- Auto-monitor citation networks; alerts when new work cites your seeds

## How to use this list

**You are a parent:** Start with the [Influencers](../influencers/) section to find clinicians whose work matches your situation, then use Google Scholar to read what they've published.

**You are a lawyer:** CourtListener + Google Scholar Case Law + BAILII (or CanLII / AustLII regionally) — search `"parental alienation"` filtered to the last 24 months for fresh authority.

**You are an evaluator or researcher:** PubMed + CrossRef + SSRN, with Zotero for capture and Connected Papers for graph navigation.

**You are a journalist:** Transparency Project + Family Law Week for UK; CourtListener oral arguments + financial disclosures for US. SSRN for academics willing to talk on record.
