# Practitioners — Argentina — Therapists

## Jurisdictional context

Argentina has no statute that names SAP/AP either to recognise it (Brazil's Lei 12.318/2010 model) or to inadmit it (Spain's LOPIVI Disp. ad. 17a model). The relevant statutory hooks are indirect: (i) Código Civil y Comercial 2014 (CCyC), art. 671 (deber de los hijos de respetar a sus padres y de no entorpecer el contacto con el otro progenitor) and the family-court régimen comunicacional regime, which generates 'AP-style' remedies without ever using the term; (ii) Ley 24.270 (1993) which criminalises impedimento de contacto by the convivient parent — long pre-existing and frequently invoked in recognition-camp argumentation; (iii) Ley 26.061 (2005) Protección Integral de los Derechos de las Niñas, Niños y Adolescentes; (iv) Ley 26.485 (2009) Protección Integral a las Mujeres, which provides the principal critique-camp counter-frame. The CSJN has not issued a Court-level apex ruling explicitly on PA/SAP. At apex civil-chamber level, the recognition-leaning line runs through the Cámara Nacional de Apelaciones en lo Civil (CNCiv), notably Sala J 'E. J. M. y otro c/ S. R. K. s/Tenencia de hijos' (1.12.2021) — characterised in Argentine commentary as the first CNCiv decision to name parental alienation without reservations — alongside earlier Sala F and Sala E lines.

The institutional critique pole is anchored by (i) FePRA (Federación de Psicólogas y Psicólogos de la República Argentina) Pronunciamiento sobre el pretendido SAP, aprobado por unanimidad en Asamblea Extraordinaria el 14.12.2019 (modificado dic 2020), invoking Art. 3.5 del Código de Ética FePRA which prohibits the application of techniques or theories without scientific endorsement; (ii) Defensoría del Pueblo de la Ciudad Autónoma de Buenos Aires Declaración del 25.9.2020 rejecting SAP for 'carecer de validez científica y, por ende, jurídica'; (iii) Defensoría del Público de Servicios de Comunicación Audiovisual recommendation against uncritical media diffusion of SAP; (iv) provincial Colegios de Psicólogos (PBA, Rosario, Mar del Plata, Córdoba, Bahía Blanca, Quilmes, La Plata) declaring SAP-grounded clinical work professionally inadmissible; (v) ASAPMI institutional position. The pre-Milei Ministerio de las Mujeres, Géneros y Diversidad and SENNAF were aligned with this pole.

2024 Milei-era restructuring is material: MMGyD was reduced to a Subsecretaría in December 2023 (Decreto 8/2023) and eliminated definitively on 6 June 2024; the Programa Nacional Las Víctimas contra las Violencias (Línea 137) was disrupted and its founder/coordinator Eva Giberti dismissed in June 2024. Giberti died on 14 December 2025 aged 96 — her entry is preserved in memoriam. The 8 November 2024 AABA repudiation of the November 2024 CEAP Congreso Internacional held at UBA Facultad de Derecho, and the Programa Género y Derecho UBA rechazo statement, are the most recent institutional critique register and post-date the federal restructuring — i.e. the critique pole has re-anchored in the universities and provincial Colegios rather than in the federal executive.

## Structural findings

- Argentina lacks a statute that names SAP/AP either to recognise or to inadmit it; the legal hook for 'AP-style' remedies is indirect via CCyC art. 671 (deber de no entorpecer el contacto) plus Ley 24.270 (impedimento de contacto, 1993). This makes Argentina structurally distinct from Brazil (Lei 12.318/2010 recognition) and Spain (LOPIVI Disp. ad. 17a inadmission) — the two LATAM/Iberian poles.
- The recognition pole is concentrated in a small Buenos Aires axis: ex-juez Eduardo Cárdenas (whose 15.9.2000 La Ley article 'El abuso de las denuncias de abuso' is the canonical introduction of SAP into Argentine judicial discourse), psiquiatras Pedro Herscovici and Carlos María Díaz Usandivaras (San Isidro/CABA family-therapy tradition), and the CEAP institutional vehicle. This is not a Cárdenas-era 'terapia interaccional familiar' tradition in the strict sense — Cárdenas's interdisciplinary judicial team predates and is methodologically distinct from the recognition-camp clinical literature that postdates his 2000 article.
- The critique pole is institutionally dense and post-1990s: FePRA Pronunciamiento (14.12.2019) explicitly bans clinical use of SAP under Art. 3.5 of its Código de Ética; provincial Colegios de Psicólogos (PBA Distrito I, Rosario, Mar del Plata, Córdoba, Bahía Blanca, Quilmes, La Plata) have declared SAP-grounded practice professionally inadmissible; ASAPMI, Defensoría del Pueblo CABA (25.9.2020) and Defensoría del Público have issued formal positions. This makes the Argentine institutional critique register comparable in density to Brazil's CFP Nota Técnica 4/2022 and Colombia's COLPSIC Posición Gremial 16.11.2023 — but routed through the colegios profesionales rather than a single federal regulator.
- The 2024 Milei restructuring (MMGyD dissolution Dec 2023 / final Jun 2024; Programa Las Víctimas disrupted; Giberti dismissed Jun 2024) shifted the institutional anchor of the critique pole away from the federal executive and into the universities (UBA Derecho Programa Género y Derecho 2024 statement; UNLP), the provincial Colegios, and civil-society professional bodies (FePRA, ASAPMI, AABA). Eva Giberti's death on 14.12.2025 closes the single most senior individual-academic critique voice; her entry is preserved in memoriam.
- Sonia Vaccaro — Argentine-born psicóloga, Madrid-based since the late 1990s, who coined 'violencia vicaria' — is the only Argentine practitioner currently active in the global critique discourse with a peer-reviewed PA-critique book (Vaccaro & Barea 2009, Desclée de Brouwer). She is primary-listed under Spain (practitioner:es.vaccaro-sonia) and cross-linked here.
- Regional variation: the recognition-camp clinical and institutional infrastructure is essentially CABA + San Isidro (Province of Buenos Aires); the critique-camp institutional infrastructure spans PBA, Santa Fe (Rosario), Córdoba, and Mendoza. Interior provinces (e.g. San Juan, Mendoza HCDMza Declaración) have legislative critique movements but thinner verifiable individual-clinician footprints than CABA.
- Argentine psiquiatra colegio numbers (matrícula) and psicóloga colegio numbers (matrícula provincial) are generally not publicly displayed on practitioner sites; verification of registration_number requires direct lookup via the relevant colegio register. This file follows the FePRA-aligned house rule of recording 'not publicly confirmed' rather than guessing.
