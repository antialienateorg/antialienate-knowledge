## Scope

Brazilian psicólogos clínicos, psicólogos jurídicos, peritos psicológicos forenses, psiquiatras forenses and clinical assistentes sociais who have either (a) worked alienação parental (AP) cases as treating clinician, perito do juízo, or assistente técnico; (b) published on AP in a peer-reviewed venue / SciELO / IBDFAM Revista Científica / CFP publication; or (c) issued a sourced public stance (recognition / critique / middle / institutional) on Lei 12.318/2010. Institutional anchors (CFP, CRPs, CFESS) are listed where the institution itself has issued a sourced doctrinal position.

## Stance codes

- `recognition` — treats AP as a real, assessable phenomenon; uses Lei 12.318/2010 framing; IBDFAM / GARI-PA / PASG-aligned; publishes perícia methods.
- `critique` — argues Lei 12.318/2010 is scientifically weak (Gardner-derived SAP), gender-biased, weaponised against DV-survivor mothers / child-sexual-abuse-disclosing children; supports CFP NT 4/2022 and / or revocation (PL 1.372/2023).
- `middle` — recognises some inter-parental conflict dynamics exist but rejects the AP/SAP frame as a diagnostic; emphasises procedural safeguards (perícia ética, Resolução CFP 11/2018 depoimento especial), best-interest evaluation without PA shortcut.
- `institutional` — federal / regional regulators or conselhos with a binding professional doctrine.

## Jurisdictional context

- **Lei nº 12.318/2010 (Lei da Alienação Parental, "LAP")** — sanctioned 26 Aug 2010 (https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/_ato2007-2010/2010/lei/l12318.htm). Defines AP as "interferência na formação psicológica da criança ou adolescente promovida ou induzida por um dos genitores...para que repudie genitor ou que cause prejuízo ao estabelecimento ou à manutenção de vínculos com este." Originated as PL 4.053/2008 (Câmara, deputado Régis de Oliveira / PSC-SP), draft elaborated by Elizio Luiz Perez (juiz do trabalho, then TRT-2/SP, later senate-side technical contributor); rapporteur no Senado Sen. Paulo Paim (PT-RS). The world's most explicit PA-recognition statute — explicitly authorises perícia psicológica or biopsicossocial multidisciplinar (art. 5º), inversão de guarda (art. 6º, VI), and (originally) suspensão de poder familiar.
- **Lei nº 14.340/2022** (https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/_ato2019-2022/2022/lei/l14340.htm) — sanctioned 18 May 2022. Removed automatic *suspensão do poder familiar* from the menu of judicial measures, made *escuta especializada / depoimento especial* (Lei 13.431/2017) mandatory under pena de nulidade, regulated visitação assistida. Critique camp reads this as partial congressional concession to the femicide/DV-silencing critique; recognition camp reads it as procedural fine-tuning that preserves the Lei.
- **CFP Nota Técnica nº 4/2022/GTEC/CG** (https://site.cfp.org.br/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/SEI_CFP-0698871-Nota-Tecnica.pdf) — process 576600003.000068/2022-53; instructs psicólogos not to ground analyses on the Lei 12.318 illicit-civil construct and explicitly recognises gender bias ("incidem predominantemente sobre mães guardiãs"). Precursor: *Debatendo sobre Alienação Parental: diferentes perspectivas* (CFP 2019, https://site.cfp.org.br/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Livro-Debatendo-sobre-Alienacao-Parental-Diferentes-Perspectivas.pdf). Origin: IX CNP (Congresso Nacional de Psicologia) 2016 Proposal 38.
- **CFESS Nota Técnica conjunta** (Dec 2022, https://www.cfess.org.br/arquivos/nota-tecnica-LAP-2022-dez.pdf) — assistentes sociais joined CFP critique.
- **PL 1.372/2023** (revogação integral da LAP) — CCJ aprovou 37-28 em 3 Dec 2025; pendente Plenário.
- **Conselhos:** Psicólogos registrados em um dos 24 **Conselhos Regionais de Psicologia (CRP)** — CRP-01 (DF), CRP-05 (RJ), CRP-06 (SP), CRP-07 (RS), CRP-08 (PR), CRP-09 (GO/TO), etc. — sob a **Conselho Federal de Psicologia (CFP)**, https://site.cfp.org.br/.
- **Perícia channels:** Varas de Família (perito do juízo), Núcleos de Atendimento à Família (NAF / NUAF) dos TJs, equipes psicossociais do Ministério Público, NACA (Núcleo de Atendimento à Criança e ao Adolescente) onde houver.

## Structural finding summary

1. **Brazil is the only LATAM jurisdiction where the institutional critique camp (CFP NT 4/2022 + CFESS joint nota) and the clinical recognition camp (IBDFAM-centred Calçada / Glicia Brazil / Trindade / Molinari / Lobo Ribeiro / Karam) are roughly equal in institutional weight.** Other LATAM jurisdictions (Colombia, Mexico, Argentina) show one camp dominant.

2. **The 2017–2019 CFP debate was substantively dialogic** — Sandra Baccara's recognition-camp chapter inside the CFP 2019 *Debatendo* volume documents this. The closure happened with NT 4/2022, not earlier; this means the "consensus" framing on either side is historically false.

3. **Lei 14.340/2022 is genuinely ambiguous** — recognition camp reads it as procedural fine-tuning that preserves the LAP; critique camp reads it as partial concession to femicide / DV-silencing critique. Both readings have textual support (removal of *suspensão do poder familiar*; mandatory *escuta especializada* under Lei 13.431/2017).

4. **A small but important "recognition-with-gender-asymmetry-concession" sub-camp exists** — Lobo Ribeiro / Karam Trindade (Vida Mental, SP) explicitly engage the 70.73%/75.6% accuser/accused gender split data on its own terms while maintaining AP can still be assessed. This breaks the binary and is the most fertile dialogue territory.

5. **Critique camp clinical roster is narrower than recognition camp**, concentrated at **UERJ + PUC-Rio** (Arantes, Torraca de Brito, Martins de Sousa) plus the Sedes Sapientiae psychoanalytic line in SP (Pisano Motta). The recognition camp has wider geographic spread (RJ + SP + RS + DF) and stronger court-employed institutional position. **Without CFP + CFESS, the clinical-practitioner critique camp would be visibly thinner.**

6. **CRP numbers are systematically under-published in BR.** Only Calçada (CRP 05/18785), Lobo Ribeiro (CREMESP 114681) and Karam Trindade (CRP 06/205826) display registry numbers publicly. Direct CRP regional lookup will be needed for outreach verification on the other 11 entries.

7. **PL 1.372/2023 (full revocation) passing CCJ 37-28 in Dec 2025 has materially raised stakes** — the recognition camp is now openly fighting to preserve LAP at the Plenário level; this directory should expect significant churn (new publications, public letters, institutional repositionings) through 2026.
