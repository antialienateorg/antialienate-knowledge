## Jurisdictional Context

Mexico is among the **highest-volume PA-clinical jurisdictions in LATAM** and the only one in the region where alienación parental was simultaneously (a) codified in a federal-entity Civil Code (CDMX), (b) repealed from that same Code, and (c) repeatedly litigated at apex-court level — producing an unusually layered doctrinal record. Any clinician practising in this space must be read against **eight load-bearing anchors**:

1. **SCJN — Acción de Inconstitucionalidad 11/2016 (Oaxaca).** Filed 2 Feb 2016 by the *Defensor de los Derechos Humanos del Pueblo de Oaxaca*. The Pleno of the SCJN, resolved partially fundada in **October 2017**; sentencia published in *Diario Oficial de la Federación* on **16 May 2018**.
   - The SCJN **invalidated** the automatic *suspensión o pérdida de la patria potestad* attached to alienación parental in Oaxaca's Código Civil arts. 336 Bis B (último párrafo), 429 Bis A (primer párrafo, "bajo pena de suspensión o pérdida de la patria potestad") and 459 fracción IV (Decreto 1380, DOF Oaxaca 2 Jan 2016).
   - It did **not** strike down the *figura* of alienación parental in toto — a frequently misreported nuance.
   - Crónica oficial: https://www.scjn.gob.mx/sites/default/files/cronicas_pleno_salas/documento/2019-08/cr-NLPH-0011-16.pdf
   - DOF: https://www.dof.gob.mx/nota_detalle.php?codigo=5522808&fecha=16/05/2018
   - Sentencia engrose: https://www2.scjn.gob.mx/juridica/engroses/3/2016/19/3_193848_3583_firmado.pdf

2. **SCJN — Amparo Directo en Revisión 2424/2014 (Primera Sala, Min. Pte. Arturo Zaldívar Lelo de Larrea).** *Status: file-number not independently verifiable in SCJN public engroses retrievable as of 2026-05-30.* The user-brief framing — that ADR 2424/2014 "confirmed that alienación parental may constitute violence against the child" — is **partially plausible** as a paraphrase of Primera-Sala doctrine (most cleanly stated in ADR 3859/2014, Min. Pte. Zaldívar, https://www.scjn.gob.mx/derechos-humanos/sites/default/files/sentencias-emblematicas/sentencia/2022-01/ADR3859-2014.pdf, which frames PA conduct as a form of *violencia familiar*) and in **ADR 2710/2017** (modificación de guarda y custodia por impedir convivencias, https://www.sitios.scjn.gob.mx/cec/sites/default/files/page/files/2020-09/ADR%202710%202017%20V.%20P%C3%BAblica%20Modificar%20Guarda%20y%20Custodia%20por%20Impedir%20Convivencias.pdf), but the specific expediente 2424/2014 is **no se ha podido verificar públicamente** in primary SCJN sources. Citers should use ADR 3859/2014 / ADR 2710/2017 / AI 11/2016 as the safer anchors.

3. **CDMX — Código Civil para el Distrito Federal, art. 323 Septimus — the 2014 → 2017 derogation saga (NOT 2021 as is sometimes reported).** This is the load-bearing correction in this file:
   - 9 May 2014: ALDF reforma adds art. 323 Septimus codifying alienación parental as form of *violencia familiar*. Texto: https://www.anasap.org/2014/05/articulo-323-septimus-del-codigo-civil-vigente-de-mexico-df/
   - 14 July 2017: ALDF approves derogación 36-0. http://www.aldf.gob.mx/comsoc-aprueba-aldf-eliminar-codigo-civil-distrito-federal-figura-alienacion-parental--34534.html
   - **4 August 2017: derogation enters into force.**
   - 1 August 2017: CDHCM (then CDHDF) celebrates derogation: https://cdhcm.org.mx/2017/08/la-cdhdf-reconoce-la-proteccion-de-los-derechos-humanos-a-traves-de-la-derogacion-de-la-mal-denominada-figura-de-alienacion-parental-del-codigo-civil-de-la-ciudad-de-mexico/
   - Cimacnoticias coverage: "Juzgados de CDMX obligados a desechar Alienación Parental" https://cimacnoticias.com.mx/noticia/juzgados-de-cdmx-obligados-a-desechar-alienacion-parental/
   - El Universal: https://www.eluniversal.com.mx/articulo/metropoli/cdmx/2017/08/1/asamblea-deroga-alienacion-parental-de-codigo-civil/
   - Subsequent reform-back attempts have occurred (notably in the 2021–2024 legislatures) but **CDMX 323 Septimus remains derogated as of 2026-05-30.**

4. **State-level civil-code variation.** Even after CDMX derogation and AI 11/2016, alienación parental remains codified or referenced in family-law provisions of: **Estado de México**, **Aguascalientes**, **Coahuila**, **Hidalgo**, **Querétaro**, **Puebla** (variable), among others. State variation is the single biggest determinant of whether a perito psicólogo encounters AP as a tipificable category in their juzgado familiar. The CELIG / Congreso CDMX (2024) *Información Técnica sobre Alienación Parental* tracks the state-code map: https://genero.congresocdmx.gob.mx/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/CELIG-Inform-Te%CC%81c-Alienacio%CC%81n-Parental-29-05-24.pdf

5. **SCJN *Protocolo para Juzgar con Perspectiva de Infancia y Adolescencia* (Nov 2021).** The doctrinal procedural anchor for jueces y peritos: requires the *interés superior de la niñez* + *perspectiva de género* transversal lens; cautions against AP as default lens but does not prohibit its consideration. https://www.scjn.gob.mx/derechos-humanos/sites/default/files/protocolos/archivos/2021-11/Protocolo%20para%20juzgar%20con%20perspectiva%20de%20IA%2010nov21.pdf

6. **CNDH — Comisión Nacional de los Derechos Humanos.** Published the multi-author institutional volume *Alienación Parental* (1ª ed. 2011, 2ª ed. expanded), 317 pp., the single most-cited Mexican institutional book on AP — explicitly mixed-positions, hosting both recognition and critique chapters. Direct URL (CNDH biblioteca): http://appweb.cndh.org.mx/biblioteca/archivos/pdfs/allienacionParental_2aEd.pdf — institutional landing https://www.cndh.org.mx/tema/194/ninez-y-familia

7. **CDHCM — Comisión de Derechos Humanos de la Ciudad de México.** Published *Alienación parental* (2015): https://cdhcm.org.mx/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Alienacion-parental.pdf. Filed *Amicus Curiae Amparo Directo 14/2020* before SCJN: https://cdhcm.org.mx/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/Amicus-Curiae-Amparo-Directo-14-2020-SCJN.pdf — the most explicit critique-side institutional intervention before the apex court.

8. **INMUJERES / CONAVIM / CEAV.** Federal women's-rights and victim-attention bodies. **No primary-source PA-specific clinical guía publicly retrievable as of 2026-05-30** from these three institutions individually, despite frequent assumption to the contrary. Their positions are inferred from the LGAMVLV (Ley General de Acceso de las Mujeres a una Vida Libre de Violencia) framework rather than from named PA-specific publications.

**Forensic structure.** Mexican family-court forensic psychology operates through three streams that practitioners must be classed against:
- **Peritos psicólogos adscritos a los Juzgados Familiares** of each state's *Poder Judicial* (CDMX, Estado de México, Jalisco, Nuevo León have the densest dockets). Reports are court-ordered.
- **Peritos del Tribunal Superior de Justicia (TSJ)** — career civil servants.
- **Peritos de parte / peritos privados** — where most named recognition-camp clinical literature is deployed.

**Direction of travel.** Mexico is **not** a critique-statute jurisdiction (it is neither Brazil's Lei 12.318/2010 recognition model nor Spain's LOPIVI Disp. ad. 17ª critique model). It is a **layered-doctrine jurisdiction**: federal apex-court invalidation of the *automaticity* of AP consequences (AI 11/2016), combined with surviving state-level codification, combined with a derogated-then-not-replaced CDMX flagship article, combined with mixed institutional positions. The recognition camp's strongest institutional anchor is **AMPFS (Asociación Mexicana de Padres de Familia Separados)** + **Mendoza-Amaro / GARI-PA**. The critique camp's strongest institutional anchors are the **CDHCM** + **CELIG/Congreso CDMX** + the academic-feminism line in **UNAM / FES Iztacala / CEIICH** + the **cimacnoticias** coverage circuit.

## Structural Finding Summary

1. **The single biggest structural finding:** **Mexico has the most layered apex-court doctrinal record on AP of any LATAM jurisdiction — and the only example of a jurisdiction that codified AP in its capital civil code (CDMX 2014), then derogated it (2017), then preserved the *figura* at the federal level while invalidating its automatic-consequence rule (SCJN AI 11/2016 / Oaxaca, ruled 2017, DOF 2018). The result is a uniquely fragmented landscape where individual clinicians on both sides operate without an institutional psicología-profession cover (unlike Brazil's CFP NT 4/2022 or Colombia's COLPSIC 2023 Posición Gremial).** The Sociedad Mexicana de Psicología has issued no PA-specific institutional position — the single most consequential negative finding in this directory.

2. **The recognition camp is institutionally healthier in Mexico than the critique camp at the *clinical-author* level**, but the inverse at the *human-rights-institution* level. Recognition is anchored by Mendoza-Amaro / AMPFS / GARI-PA (clinician + advocacy + international-network triad); critique is anchored by CDHCM + CELIG + cimacnoticias (institutional / press / academic triad) but is **thin on named individual clinician-authors** with primary PA publications. The two FES Iztacala figures (Laura Evelia Torres = recognition; Patricia Trujano = critique-adjacent) are the cleanest illustration: same campus, opposite sides, both legitimate.

3. **CDMX 323 Septimus chronology was significantly mis-dated in the briefing-set assumption.** Derogation was 2017, not "nearly repealed 2021". The post-2017 reform attempts (Iniciativa Morena vs PAN/PRI dynamics of 2021–2024) are *re-introduction* attempts against the derogated status quo. Any directory entry on Mexico that uses 2021 as the load-bearing CDMX date is wrong.

4. **SCJN AI 11/2016 (Oaxaca) is the single load-bearing apex-court anchor for Mexican PA jurisprudence**, not ADR 2424/2014 (which is not independently verifiable as a primary source). ADR 3859/2014 (Min. Pte. Zaldívar) and ADR 2710/2017 are the two most-quoted Primera Sala companions. Substitute these citations forward.

5. **State-code variation matters operationally more than federal doctrine.** Even with CDMX derogation, AP remains in the Civil Codes of Estado de México, Aguascalientes, Coahuila, Hidalgo, Querétaro, Puebla. The same perito psicólogo working a custodia-impugnada case will encounter AP as a tipificable category in Toluca and not in CDMX — across the same Valle de México metropolis. This is the under-reported operational fact pattern in Mexican PA-clinical work.

6. **CNDH 2011 *Alienación Parental* (2nd edition, 317 pp.) is the single most operationally-useful Mexican institutional reference** — explicitly mixed-positions, used by both camps. Any practitioner directory in this jurisdiction that omits the CNDH volume is incomplete.
