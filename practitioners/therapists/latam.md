# LATAM Therapists & Clinicians — Parental Alienation Practitioner Directory

**Schema version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-05-25
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (intended for github.com/AntiAlienate/antialienate-knowledge)
**Region:** LATAM (BR / MX / AR / CO / CL)
**Scope:** Latin-American psychiatrists, psychologists (clinical / forensic / academic), psicólogos peritos, and clinical social workers who have (a) worked PA cases as treating clinician or court-appointed expert (perito forense / perito judicial), (b) published on alienação parental / alienación parental in a peer-reviewed venue or recognised professional publication, or (c) publicly stated a sourced stance (recognition / critique / middle) on PA. Institutional entries (national psychology councils, national child-protection bodies, victim-services networks) are listed where the institution itself has issued a sourced PA position.

**Standards:** Primary sources only — national register entries where searchable, practice / institutional website, peer-reviewed publications, court records, named press. Where a fact could not be confirmed from a public primary source it is flagged "**not publicly confirmed**".

**Stance taxonomy:**
- `recognition` — treats alienação parental / alienación parental as a real, assessable phenomenon; operates or endorses PA-specific assessment, expert-witness or reunification work; typically PASG / CEAP / GARI-PA-aligned.
- `critique` — argues the PA construct (especially Gardner's SAP) is operationalised against DV / coercive-control survivors (predominantly mothers and children); advocates DV/trauma-informed alternatives and tighter regulation of expert witnesses; supports repeal of Lei 12.318/2010 or jurisprudential proscription as in Colombia 2023.
- `middle` — engages both alienating-behaviour and family-violence frames; differential-diagnosis or systemic / multi-family approach; not aligned with PASG and not in the abolitionist critique camp.

**Critical LATAM regulatory & policy context (must read before using this directory):**

- **Brazil (BR):** Psychologists are registered with one of 24 **Conselhos Regionais de Psicologia (CRP)** (e.g. CRP-RJ 05, CRP-SP 06) under the **Conselho Federal de Psicologia (CFP)**, https://site.cfp.org.br/. The 2022 **CFP Nota Técnica nº 4/2022/GTEC/CG** (https://site.cfp.org.br/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/SEI_CFP-0698871-Nota-Tecnica.pdf) is the institutional doctrinal anchor: it instructs psicólogos *not* to ground analyses on Lei 12.318/2010 as a clinical construct, and recognises PA allegations as predominantly affecting guardian mothers with a gender bias. IBDFAM and a substantial recognition-camp clinical literature (Calçada, Brazil, Perissini) push back. Lei 12.318/2010 + Lei 14.340/2022 remain in force; CCJ approved PL 1.372/2023 (full revocation) 37-28 on 3 Dec 2025.
- **Mexico (MX):** Psicólogos hold a **cédula profesional** issued by the **Dirección General de Profesiones / SEP** (https://www.cedulaprofesional.sep.gob.mx/); there is no single binding national colegio of psychologists. State civil codes diverge on *alienación parental* — CDMX (Civil Code reform 2022), Estado de México and others incorporate it; **SCJN Acción de Inconstitucionalidad 11/2016 (Oaxaca)** struck down the criminal/loss-of-custody automaticity. The 2021 **SCJN Protocolo para Juzgar con Perspectiva de Infancia y Adolescencia** (https://www.scjn.gob.mx/derechos-humanos/sites/default/files/protocolos/archivos/2021-11/Protocolo%20para%20juzgar%20con%20perspectiva%20de%20IA%2010nov21.pdf) is the doctrinal anchor.
- **Argentina (AR):** Psicólogos register with provincial colegios (e.g. **Colegio de Psicólogos de la Provincia de Buenos Aires** https://www.colpsiba.org.ar/, **CPCBA** for CABA https://www.psicologos.org.ar/); psiquiatras with the **APSA / Asociación Argentina de Psiquiatras** and provincial colegios médicos. **Ley 24.270** criminalises obstruction of contact (1993) but does not codify SAP. **ASAPMI** (https://asapmi.org.ar/) is the leading professional society publishing the critique-camp position; **CEAP Argentina** (https://alienacionparental.com.ar/) is the recognition-camp institutional pole.
- **Colombia (CO):** Psicólogos register with **COLPSIC – Colegio Colombiano de Psicólogos** (https://www.colpsic.org.co/) and obtain the **tarjeta profesional**. The institutional doctrine pivoted in **November 2023**: COLPSIC issued *Posición Gremial sobre Alienación Parental* (16 Nov 2023 — https://www.colpsic.org.co/comunicados/posicion-del-colegio-colombiano-de-psicologos-colpsic-frente-al-concepto-de-alienacion-parental/), and on **30 Nov 2023** the **Corte Constitucional Sentencia T-526/23** (https://www.corteconstitucional.gov.co/relatoria/2023/t-526-23.htm) proscribed SAP as a criterion for judicial analysis of children's testimony. The **ICBF – Instituto Colombiano de Bienestar Familiar** (https://www.icbf.gov.co/) is the institutional family-court psychology body referenced in T-526.
- **Chile (CL):** Psicólogos register with the **Colegio de Psicólogos de Chile** (https://www.colegiopsicologos.cl/) — collegiate membership is voluntary post-1981. **SENAME / mejor niñez** (https://www.mejorninez.cl/) is the national child-protection service. There is **no entity in Chile that institutionally addresses alienación parental** and it is neither a crime nor a clinical diagnosis in the Chilean health system; the principal clinical voices are individual academics (Pinto-Cortez at UTA; Portilla-Saavedra and Moya-Vergara at UCN) and the **Instituto Chileno de Terapia Familiar (IChTF)** for the middle / family-systems camp.
