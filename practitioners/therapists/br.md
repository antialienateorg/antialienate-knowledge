Brazil is unique among LATAM5 jurisdictions for having ROUGHLY EQUAL institutional weight at both poles: statutory recognition (Lei 12.318/2010) + STJ apex recognition line vs CFP+CFESS professional-association critique. ## Critique camp

### Conselho Federal de Psicologia (CFP)
*Statutory federal regulator of psychology in Brazil under Lei 5.766/1971; coordinating body of 24 Conselhos Regionais de Psicologia (CRPs); statutory psychology licensure*
Brasília · Distrito Federal · Conselho Federal de Psicologia — federal statutory psychology regulator

**Stance:** INSTITUTIONAL CRITIQUE-CAMP ANCHOR for Brazilian PA-adjacent landscape. CFP NOTA TÉCNICA 4/2022 explicitly rejects the parental-alienation construct as a clinical category and warns psychologists against deploying SAP/PA-construct in family-court evaluations. The Nota Técnica 4/2022 is the principal Brazilian regulator-level critique-camp document and is regularly cited by Brazilian family-court advocacy challenging Lei 12.318/2010-grounded reasoning. CFP position is distinct from STJ apex line (REsp 1859228/SP + REsp 2108750/GO 2024 procedural-application recognition) — Brazil is the only LATAM5 jurisdiction where critique and recognition poles operate at roughly equal institutional weight.

### Conselho Federal de Serviço Social (CFESS)
*Statutory federal regulator of social work in Brazil under Lei 8.662/1993; coordinating body of 27 Conselhos Regionais de Serviço Social (CRESS); statutory social work licensure*
Brasília · Distrito Federal · Conselho Federal de Serviço Social — federal statutory social work regulator

**Stance:** PARALLEL INSTITUTIONAL CRITIQUE-CAMP ANCHOR to CFP. CFESS has issued institutional positions parallel to CFP NT 4/2022 questioning the PA-construct deployment in family-court contexts — particularly in cases where alienating-construct framing risks obscuring underlying domestic violence dynamics. CFESS-registered social workers act as forensic assessors and técnicos in Brazilian family-court proceedings under Estatuto da Criança e do Adolescente (ECA, Lei 8.069/1990) — CFESS institutional position constrains regulated practice. Joint CFP+CFESS pole is the principal Brazilian regulator-level critique anchor against Lei 12.318/2010 application.

## Institutional anchors

### Instituto Brasileiro de Direito de Família (IBDFAM)
*Voluntary academic-professional association of Brazilian family-law practitioners and academics; founded 1997; coordinating body across Brazilian universities and family-law practice*
Belo Horizonte · Minas Gerais · Instituto Brasileiro de Direito de Família — academic-professional family-law association

**Stance:** LOAD-BEARING LEGAL-ACADEMIC ANCHOR on Brazilian family-law doctrine including PA doctrine. IBDFAM positions inform STJ case-law engagement with Lei 12.318/2010 + Constitution 1988 art. 227 (children's-rights paramountcy). Unlike CFP and CFESS which carry explicit critique-camp positions, IBDFAM operates as an academic-doctrinal aggregator across family-law practice — its institutional commentary engages both recognition-camp procedural application of Lei 12.318/2010 and critique-camp methodological concerns. IBDFAM congresses (Congresso Nacional de Direito de Família) include AP-doctrine panels that have been the principal Brazilian academic forum for the construct debate. Classified institutional rather than recognition or critique on the strength of methodological aggregator role.
