# Practitioners — LATAM (BR/MX/AR/CO/CL) — Lawyers

## Jurisdictional context

This file consolidates Latin-American family-law practitioners (Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia, Chile) who have appeared as named counsel/promovente in apex PA litigation, published in the field at scholarly/book-length level, chaired an official PA-related expert body, or publicly stated a sourced stance through institutional advocacy. LATAM is the only region in which one jurisdiction (Brazil) operates under a statute that names and recognises parental alienation (Lei 12.318/2010), while the four Spanish-speaking apex courts of the region have consolidated against PA recognition between 2017 and 2025 — making the LATAM legal-practice axis structurally bipolar.

Brazil: STJ has produced a recognition-leaning family-law line (REsp 1.859.228/SP Nancy Andrighi 2024; REsp 2.108.750/GO 2024) and STF dismissed the first constitutional challenge to Lei 12.318/2010 (ADI 6273/2019, Rosa Weber, 17.12.2021, lack of standing — merits untouched). IBDFAM (Instituto Brasileiro de Direito de Família) is the institutional recognition anchor (Pereira, Madaleno, Rosa, Dias-as-middle); Themis / AAIG / MPSP-GEVID constitute the critique pole; UN SR Reem Alsalem's 2022 and 2025 calls for revocation of Lei 12.318 sit at the centre of the live legislative debate (PL 1.372/2023 repeal, PL 2812/22).

Mexico: SCJN Acción de Inconstitucionalidad 11/2016 (Pleno, Piña Hernández, 26.10.2017) invalidated Oaxaca Decreto 1380 inserting 'alienación parental' into the state civil code; the CDMX statutory equivalent (Art. 323 Septimus Código Civil del Distrito Federal) was derogated by reform in October 2017 (Decreto publicado Gaceta Oficial CDMX 22.6.2017 / posterior). The doctrine was extended in AI 74/2024 (Pleno, 29.4.2025) invalidating Tlaxcala Art. 168 Ter ¶4. Named promoventes: Arturo Peimbert Calvo (DDHPO Oaxaca, 2016) and María Estela Ríos González (CJEF, 2024). Institutional critique anchors: Equis Justicia para las Mujeres, Intersecta.

Argentina: CSJN has issued no apex PA-specific ruling. The recognition line operates through CNCiv (Sala J, 'E.J.M. y otro c/ S.R.K. s/Tenencia de hijos', 1.12.2021 — characterised in commentary as the first CNCiv decision to name AP without reservations; CSJN 7-X-2021 P.B., E.G. c/ B., K.E. (CSJ 1813/2018/RH1) on medidas precautorias). The institutional critique pole runs through ABOFEM Argentina, ASAPMI, FePRA Pronunciamiento 14.12.2019, the provincial Colegios de Psicólogos and the Defensoría del Pueblo CABA (25.9.2020). Recognition-camp doctrinal anchor: Mauricio Luis Mizrahi (CEAP / UBA).

Colombia: Corte Constitucional Sentencia T-526/2023 (Sala Tercera de Revisión, Diana Fajardo Rivera ponente, 30.11.2023) is the widest LATAM apex-court PA-proscription ruling and the strongest comparative reference point alongside the Italian Cassazione 9691/2022 anti-PAS line. The institutional critique coalition is led by Corporación Sisma Mujer (1998-; director Linda Cabrera) which filed the 2024 CIDH petition documenting five Colombian SAP-related cases against DV-survivor mothers. ABOFEM Colombia operates as the second-line critique vehicle.

Chile: Boletín 10.516-18 (proyecto que reconocía SAP como acto constitutivo de violencia intrafamiliar) was rejected in April 2024 after sustained institutional opposition from Corporación Humanas, ABOFEM Chile and the Corte Suprema (Ministra Andrea Muñoz, Secretaría Técnica de Igualdad de Género). The substituted text — Ley 21.675/2024 (publicada 14.6.2024) — recognises 'violencia vicaria' as an aggravated form of violence against women and replaces the SAP-as-VIF framing. Patsilí Toledo Vásquez (Chilean academic in diaspora at Universidad Pompeu Fabra Barcelona; CEDAW Committee member 2025-2028) is the senior comparative-law voice anchoring the Chilean critique pole internationally.

## Structural findings

- 20 verified entries across 5 countries (BR 8 / MX 4 / AR 4 / CO 2 / CL 2). Brazil is the only LATAM jurisdiction with a PA-recognition statute (Lei 12.318/2010); the other four Spanish-speaking apex courts of the region have consolidated against PA recognition between 2017 and 2025.
- Apex-court consolidation against PA recognition 2017-2025 (outside Brazil): SCJN Mexico AI 11/2016 (26.10.2017) + AI 74/2024 (29.4.2025); Corte Constitucional Colombia T-526/2023 (30.11.2023); Chile Boletín 10.516-18 rejected April 2024 with statutory substitution by Ley 21.675/2024 (violencia vicaria framework); Argentina CSJN no apex PA ruling but CNCiv Sala J 1.12.2021 first to name AP — recognition operates at civil-chamber level only.
- LATAM legal-practice axis = IBDFAM recognition (BR institutional) vs ABOFEM (regional) / Themis (BR) / Sisma Mujer (CO) / ASAPMI (AR) / Humanas (CL) critique coalition. This is the most institutionally articulated bipolar structure in the region. The recognition pole is concentrated in Brazil (IBDFAM-led, ~4 of 8 BR entries); the critique pole spans all five jurisdictions.
- Brazil STJ family-law line is recognition-leaning (REsp 1.859.228/SP Andrighi 2024; REsp 2.108.750/GO 2024) and STF has not ruled on the merits of Lei 12.318/2010 (ADI 6273/2019 dismissed for lack of standing 17.12.2021, Rel. Min. Rosa Weber). Counsel of record in STJ family-law decisions is anonymised by privacy convention — direct named-counsel attribution at apex level is concentrated on institutional promoventes (Themis ADI 6273) and doctrinal authors (Dias, Madaleno, Pereira, Rosa).
- Mexico apex doctrine is anchored on two SCJN Acciones de Inconstitucionalidad with publicly named promoventes: Peimbert Calvo (AI 11/2016, Oaxaca, 2017) and Ríos González (AI 74/2024, Tlaxcala, 2025). The CDMX equivalent (Art. 323 Septimus) was derogated by reform 2017 without need for SCJN intervention. The named-promovente structure makes Mexican PA constitutional litigation the most directly addressable critique-camp infrastructure in LATAM.
- Argentina recognition anchor is doctrinal (Mizrahi, La Ley 12.4.2022; Astrea book 2022; UBA Derecho 2024 Congreso) routed through CNCiv civil chambers rather than CSJN apex. The CSJN 7-X-2021 P.B., E.G. ruling addressed medidas precautorias procedural questions, not PA recognition on the merits. ABOFEM Argentina + ASAPMI + FePRA constitute the institutional critique infrastructure.
- Colombia T-526/23 is the strongest LATAM PA-proscription apex ruling and the natural comparative citation anchor alongside Italian Cassazione 9691/2022 (sentenza Acierno) and German BVerfG 1 BvR 1076/23 (2023). Corporación Sisma Mujer (Linda Cabrera) is the institutional architect; ABOFEM Colombia operates as second-line vehicle.
- Chile lacks a single anchor apex-court PA ruling comparable to MX 11/2016 or CO T-526/23. The institutional critique pole operates legislatively (Boletín 10.516-18 rejection April 2024 + Ley 21.675/2024 violencia vicaria substitution) rather than judicially. Patsilí Toledo Vásquez (Pompeu Fabra Barcelona / CEDAW 2025-2028) is the senior comparative-law diaspora voice.
- Critique-camp named-counsel verification: Alsalem 2022 + 2025 UN SR calls for revocation of Lei 12.318 draw on collective AAIG/Themis/feminist-coalition work plus Câmara feminist deputies (Melchionna, Bomfim, Reis on PL 2812/22) rather than a single named individual advocate. The institutional structure of LATAM PA critique is collective and institutional rather than personality-led.
- Apex-court counsel anonymisation: BR (STJ), AR (CSJN, SCBA), CO (Corte Constitucional T-526/23 tutela) anonymise child parties and frequently their counsel. Named-counsel entries are concentrated on (a) constitutional-action promoventes where standing is institutional and (b) doctrinal-commentary authors.
- PUC Chile / Centro UC de la Familia (Carmen Domínguez): substantial family-law output but PA-specific publication not surfaced in available web sources at directory level — Spanish-language academic-library pass would likely identify a PA piece. Documented in excluded entries for future re-evaluation.
- Mexico CDMX private practice: PA jurisprudence dominated by institutional / constitutional-action lawyers. Private-practice family lawyers handling individual SCJN amparo cases not retrievable from public-source searches at directory level.
- Brazil IBDFAM/RS leadership transitioned September 2025 (Conrado Paulino da Rosa removed by IBDFAM National Board 19.9.2025; firm role unaffected per IBDFAM Nota Oficial). Entry preserved with note flagging the institutional transition.
- Eduardo Cárdenas (AR) confusion in v1: the original brief listed him as 'ASAPMI president' — primary-source check confirmed Cárdenas was an ex-Juez de Familia whose 2000 La Ley article 'El abuso de la denuncia de abuso' introduced SAP/PA into Argentine practitioner discourse. ASAPMI's institutional stance is opposite. Cárdenas's role is historical-doctrinal and is preserved under the AR therapists file (ar.cardenas-eduardo).
- Sonia Vaccaro — Argentine-born, Madrid-based, coiner of 'violencia vicaria' — is the only Argentine practitioner currently active in global PA-critique discourse with a peer-reviewed book (Vaccaro & Barea 2009, Desclée de Brouwer). Primary-listed under Spain therapists, cross-referenced from AR therapists. The 'violencia vicaria' frame she developed is the doctrinal hinge of Chile's Ley 21.675/2024 substitution of SAP-as-VIF.
- Cross-jurisdictional doctrinal traffic 2023-2025: Corte Constitucional Colombia T-526/23 → cited by Diario Constitucional Chile (16.2.2024); Italian Cassazione 9691/2022 (Acierno) → reciprocally cited by Colombian and Chilean commentary; German BVerfG 1 BvR 1076/23 (2023) → comparative anchor for European/LATAM critique convergence.
- Recognition pole has not produced comparable cross-LATAM citation traffic. IBDFAM (Brazil) doctrine is country-specific to Lei 12.318/2010; Mizrahi (AR) doctrine is country-specific to CCyC 2014 + Ley 24.270. No regional recognition-camp institutional vehicle equivalent to ABOFEM or Sisma Mujer.
- Femicidio/violencia vicaria substitution as critique-camp legislative strategy: Chile Ley 21.675/2024 is the regional pilot. Spain LOPIVI Disp. ad. 17a (2021) inadmission and Italy Cassazione 9691/2022 jurisprudential line are precedents. LATAM jurisdictions other than Brazil are tracking the European critique trajectory.
- Tier 1 named-counsel interlocutor density: BR 4 (Dias, Madaleno, Pereira, Cruz/Themis), MX 2 (Peimbert, Ríos), AR 1 (Mizrahi), CO 2 (Cabrera/Sisma, Fajardo magistrada — institutional only), CL 0 individual at apex (Muñoz as institutional). Reflects the asymmetric LATAM institutional architecture.
- Honest gap: critique-camp Brazilian apex-counsel attribution beyond Cruz/Themis (ADI 6273/2019). Public-record analysis identifies AAIG/Themis collective work and Câmara feminist-deputy bloc (Melchionna, Bomfim, Reis) but no single named advocate equivalent to Mizrahi (recognition AR) or Peimbert (critique MX). Preserved as honest gap rather than fabricated entry.
