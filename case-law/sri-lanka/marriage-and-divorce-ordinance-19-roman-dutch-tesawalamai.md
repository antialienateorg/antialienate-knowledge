## TL;DR

Sri Lanka operates a **plural family-law system** combining colonial Roman-Dutch general law (Marriage and Divorce Ordinance), the Tamil-region **Tesawalamai** (Jaffna customary law for Sri Lankan Tamils), and the **Muslim Marriage and Divorce Act** for Sri Lankan Muslims. Hague 1980 + Hague 1996 **non-signatory**. Substantial diaspora in UK (~250k Tamil + Sinhalese), Canada (~150k), Australia, India creates frequent cross-border custody issues with no Hague framework.

## Statutory framework

### General Marriage and Divorce Ordinance (No 19 of 1907, as amended)
- Applies to Sri Lankan Sinhalese, Burghers, others
- Best interest of child standard in custody disputes
- Sole or joint custody possible per court order

### Tesawalamai
- Customary law of Jaffna Tamils (codified by the Dutch in 1707)
- Distinctive property + matrimonial framework
- Applies in conjunction with general law

### Muslim Marriage and Divorce Act (No 13 of 1951, as amended)
- Applies to Sri Lankan Muslims
- Quazi (Muslim judge) jurisdiction
- Hanafi-influenced framework

### Children and Young Persons Ordinance + Kandyan Law
- Kandyan customary law applies to Kandyan Sinhalese
- Children's framework supplements personal laws

## Supreme Court jurisprudence

### Multiple welfare-of-child decisions
- Best interest standard consistently applied across personal-law frameworks
- Welfare overlay reduces conflict between legal systems

## Hague + cross-border framework

- **Non-signatory** to Hague 1980 + Hague 1996
- Bilateral treaties limited
- USDOS classifies as country of concern for parental child abduction
- UK-Sri Lanka, Canada-Sri Lanka, Australia-Sri Lanka — significant cross-border family-law caseload via diaspora

## Diaspora pattern

- **UK**: ~250k (Tamil + Sinhalese)
- **Canada**: ~150k (Toronto Tamil community largest in West)
- **Australia**: ~110k
- **India**: substantial Tamil cross-border
- **Switzerland, Germany**: post-1983 refugee waves
