## TL;DR

Finland's family-law framework comprises the **Marriage Act 234/1929** (amended extensively) plus the **Child Custody and Right of Access Act 361/1983** (Laki lapsen huollosta ja tapaamisoikeudesta). Joint custody is the strong post-divorce default. 2019 reform strengthened shared-residence framework. Hague 1980 (1994) + Hague 1996 (2011) + Brussels IIb. Nordic Cooperation Convention framework simplifies bilateral with Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Iceland.

## Statutory framework

### Child Custody and Right of Access Act 361/1983
- s.1: best interest of child paramount
- s.7: joint custody framework
- s.9: post-divorce — joint custody continues unless court otherwise
- s.9a (2019 amendment): shared residence (vuoroasuminen) framework
- s.10: contact right and facilitation duty

### Marriage Act 234/1929
- Divorce framework
- Coordination with Child Custody Act

## Supreme Court of Finland (Korkein oikeus) jurisprudence

### KKO 2021:32
- Application of post-2019 shared-residence framework
- Welfare paramountcy

### Multiple PA-citing decisions (post-2019)
- Vanhempien välinen vieraannuttaminen (parental alienation) recognised

## Hague + Brussels framework

- **Hague 1980**: signatory since 1 Aug 1994; **Ministry of Justice** is CA
- **Hague 1996**: signatory since 1 Mar 2012
- **Brussels IIb** (Reg. 2019/1111): intra-EU framework
- **Nordic Cooperation Convention**: simplified bilateral with SE, NO, DK, IS

## Parental alienation recognition

- Vanhempien välinen vieraannuttaminen recognised in Supreme Court jurisprudence
- s.10 facilitation duty + welfare-paramount framework provide statutory hooks
- Finnish Bar Association PA practitioner guidance 2022

## Diaspora pattern

- **Estonia**: ~30k (proximity)
