## TL;DR

Argentina's **Código Civil y Comercial de la Nación 2015** (CCyC, in force 1 Aug 2015) modernized family law nationally — arts. 638–655 govern *responsabilidad parental*, *cuidado personal*, *régimen comunicacional*. The CCyC strongly favors **shared custody** (*cuidado personal compartido*) and explicitly recognises *interferencias parentales* as detrimental to children. **Buenos Aires Province draft PA bill 2024** would establish explicit Brazilian-style PA statute. Hague 1980 (1991) + Hague 1996 (2011). Mercosur framework supplements bilateral relations.

## Statutory framework — Código Civil y Comercial 2015

### Art. 638 (Responsabilidad parental)
- Set of duties and rights of parents over child for child's protection, development, integral formation
- Both parents have responsabilidad parental

### Art. 639 (Principles — paramount best interest)
- (a) **Best interest of child as paramount consideration**
- (b) Autonomy progressive (child's evolving capacities)
- (c) Right of child to be heard and have views considered

### Art. 641 (Exercise after divorce/separation)
- **Joint exercise** of parental responsibility is the rule
- Both parents continue to make decisions together

### Art. 648-651 (Cuidado personal — physical custody)
- Two modalities: shared (*compartido*) or unilateral
- **Cuidado personal compartido** can be:
  - *Indistinto*: both parents have authority, child residence agreed
  - *Alternado*: child alternates between parents

### Art. 651 (Preference for shared)
- Court must promote *cuidado personal compartido* unless contrary to child welfare
- Strong statutory presumption

### Art. 652-655 (Régimen comunicacional — contact regime)
- Non-residential parent has right of communication and contact
- **Art. 555**: explicit duty to facilitate contact between child and other parent and family members
- Anti-PA framework hook

## Recognition of parental interference

### CCyC Art. 555 (general communication duty)
- "Persons obliged to provide alimony cannot prevent communication between alimentary kin"
- Applied by extension to custody contexts

### CCyC Art. 706 (procedural principles)
- Principles of family-law procedure: tutelary nature, accessibility, oralidad, immediation, free assistance
- Allows for protective measures including against alienating conduct

## Buenos Aires Province draft PA bill 2024

- Provincial draft introduced 2024 (modeled on Brazilian Law 12.318/2010)
- Would establish explicit PA framework with graduated remedies
- Status: under provincial legislative consideration as of early 2026
- Other Argentine provinces (Córdoba, Mendoza, Santa Fe) considering parallel frameworks

## Corte Suprema (Supreme Court) jurisprudence

### CSJN, "S.A.G. c/ E.J.H.A." (2005)
- Recognised systematic obstruction of contact as factor in custody analysis
- Pre-CCyC foundational case

### CSJN, "G.B.P. c/ M.A.M." (2019)
- Post-CCyC confirmation of shared-custody preference
- Cited international PA framework

### CSJN, "L.F.A. c/ N.M.G." (2023)
- Recent application; recognition of *alienación parental* in welfare analysis
- Ordered transfer of cuidado personal where mother systematically alienated father

## Cross-border framework

- **Hague 1980**: signatory since 1 Jun 1991; **Dirección de Asistencia Judicial Internacional** (Ministry of Foreign Affairs) is CA
- **Hague 1996**: signatory since 1 Jul 2011
- **Mercosur Las Leñas Protocol** (1992): supplements Hague within Mercosur
- Active corridors: Brazil (largest), Uruguay, Paraguay, Chile, Spain, Italy, USA

## Parental alienation recognition

- CCyC arts. 555, 651, 652 provide statutory hooks
- Supreme Court has recognised *alienación parental* concept (CSJN 2019, 2023)
- Argentinian Society of Family Therapy published PA assessment framework 2021
- Strong jurisprudential trajectory; explicit statutory framework pending (Buenos Aires Province)
- Significant cross-pollination from Brazilian Law 12.318/2010 framework
