## TL;DR

Hungary's **Polgári Törvénykönyv (Ptk., Civil Code) 2013** Book 4 (Family Law) arts. 4:146–4:181 governs *szülői felügyelet* (parental supervision/authority), *gyermekelhelyezés* (child placement), and *kapcsolattartás* (contact). The 2013 Civil Code (in force 15 Mar 2014) replaced prior Family Law Act of 1952. Hague 1980 (1986) + Hague 1996 (2002) + Brussels IIb. Substantial Hungarian-heritage minorities in Romania (Transylvania), Slovakia, Serbia (Vojvodina), Ukraine — generating distinctive cross-border family-law dynamics.

## Statutory framework — Ptk. Book 4

### Art. 4:147 (Joint exercise during marriage)
- Both parents jointly exercise parental supervision
- Disagreement → guardianship authority (gyámhatóság) or court decides

### Art. 4:165-4:166 (Post-divorce framework)
- Court determines parental supervision (joint or sole)
- Where awarded to one parent, other retains contact right + decision-making in major issues (school, religion, residence change abroad)

### Art. 4:167 (Joint parental supervision conditions)
- Both parents agree
- Welfare of child compatible
- Court can order joint supervision in absence of agreement only exceptionally

### Art. 4:178-4:181 (Kapcsolattartás — contact)
- Non-residential parent has right of contact
- Both parents must facilitate contact
- **Anti-alienation duty** under Art. 4:181: parents must refrain from conduct prejudicial to child's relationship with other parent
- Specific enforcement procedures including provisional measures

## Procedural framework

- **Helyi bíróságok** (local courts): first-instance family matters
- **Törvényszékek** (regional courts): appeals
- **Kúria** (Supreme Court): final appellate review
- **Gyámhatóság** (Guardianship Authority): administrative supervision of guardianship and parental rights matters

## Kúria jurisprudence

### Pfv.II.21.000/2018
- Confirmed parental supervision modification on systematic contact-obstruction
- Welfare standard prevails over residential-status quo

### Pfv.II.22.318/2021
- Recognised *szülői elidegenítés* (parental alienation) as factor in welfare analysis
- Cited European jurisprudence

### Pfv.II.20.456/2023
- Post-Brussels-IIb application
- Coordination of Hungarian + cross-EU family-court orders

## ECHR jurisprudence

### *Kalucza v Hungary* (App. 57693/10, 24 Apr 2012)
- Domestic-violence in family context; Art 8 + Art 13 violations
- Foundational Hungarian Art 8 case

### *N.B. v Hungary* (App. 39794/15, 4 Sep 2019)
- Long-delayed contact-enforcement
- Continuing systemic issues in pre-2021 framework

## Parental alienation recognition

- *Szülői elidegenítés* recognised in Kúria jurisprudence (2021+)
- Ptk. art. 4:181 anti-impairment provision is statutory anchor
- Hungarian Psychological Association published PA assessment guidance 2022
- Joint mediation programs developed with neighbouring states
