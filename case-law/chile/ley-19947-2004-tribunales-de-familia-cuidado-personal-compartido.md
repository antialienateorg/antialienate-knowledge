## TL;DR

Chile's family-law framework comprises **Ley 19.947 (2004)** on civil marriage, **Ley 19.968 (2004)** establishing specialised Family Courts (Tribunales de Familia), and the **2013 reform** introducing *cuidado personal compartido* (shared custody) as preferred regime. Hague 1980 (1994) + Hague 1996 (2017). Active corridors with USA, Argentina, Peru, Bolivia, Spain.

## Statutory framework

### Ley 19.947/2004 (Ley de Matrimonio Civil)
- Modern civil divorce framework
- Best-interest paramountcy

### Ley 19.968/2004 (Tribunales de Familia)
- Established specialized Family Courts
- Mandatory mediation in custody disputes
- Multi-disciplinary court framework

### Código Civil arts. 222-244 (Patria potestad)
- 2013 amendment introduced *cuidado personal compartido* as preferred regime
- Joint custody where parents agree; court can order
- Both parents retain decision-making authority

### Ley 19.620 (Adoption Law)
- Children's protective framework

## Corte Suprema de Chile jurisprudence

### Rol N° 25.471-2018
- Application of 2013 shared-custody preference
- Welfare-of-child paramount

### Multiple post-2018 PA-citing decisions
- Recognition of *interferencias parentales* concept
- Cross-pollination from Argentine + Brazilian frameworks

## Hague + cross-border framework

- **Hague 1980**: signatory since 1 May 1994; **Servicio Nacional de Menores (SENAME) / Mejor Niñez (post-2021)** is CA
- **Hague 1996**: signatory since 1 Mar 2018
- Active corridors: USA, Argentina, Peru, Bolivia, Spain, Italy

## Parental alienation recognition

- Welfare-paramount framework permits PA-evidence
- Tribunales de Familia increasingly cite *interferencias parentales*
- Chilean Bar Association (Colegio de Abogados) PA practitioner guidance 2022
- Cross-pollination with Argentine and Brazilian frameworks

## Diaspora pattern

- **Argentina**: largest single overseas community
- **USA**: ~150k Chilean-American
- **Spain**: substantial
- **Italy, Sweden** (post-1973 refugee waves and descendants)
