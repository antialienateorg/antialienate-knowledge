# Tribunal Supremo (Spain) — Coached Refusal + Alienating Behaviors Doctrine Line

> **TL;DR.** Spanish Tribunal Supremo and provincial-court (Audiencias Provinciales) jurisprudence has converged on the same international pattern: **reject "PAS" as a diagnosable syndrome, accept "alienating behaviors" (conductas alienadoras) as legally cognizable, and require courts to investigate the origin of a child's refusal of contact**. Anchored by LO 8/2021 (Spain's child-protection statute) and Código Civil Art. 92 + 94 + 158 (medidas para el cumplimiento del régimen de visitas).

## Key statutory framework

### Código Civil

- **Art. 92** — patria potestad + custodia compartida preference
- **Art. 94** — derecho de visita; courts may modify based on child's interest
- **Art. 158** — *medidas necesarias* — court can adopt any measure to ensure compliance, including astreintes-style daily penalties

### LO 8/2021 — Ley Orgánica de protección integral a la infancia y la adolescencia frente a la violencia

Article 26 explicitly addresses **violencia vicaria** (proxy violence) — recognized as a form of family violence where one parent harms the child to harm the other parent. This is the Spanish statutory hook for alienation-as-family-violence (consistent with Harman/Kruk/Hines 2018).

## The doctrinal line

| Element | Spanish position |
|---|---|
| PAS as diagnosis | Rejected — no syndromal recognition |
| Alienating behaviors | Accepted — *conductas alienadoras* legally cognizable |
| Coached refusal | Court must investigate source — cannot rubber-stamp |
| Violencia vicaria | Statutorily named (LO 8/2021 Art. 26) |
| Compliance enforcement | Art. 158 CC + astreintes — court has broad authority |
| Modification of custody | Available where alienation pattern documented |
| ECHR Art. 8 integration | Direct via *bloc de constitucionalidad* + Art. 10.2 CE |

## Significance for PA litigation in Spain

Spanish targeted parents have **stronger statutory hooks** than several neighboring jurisdictions:

1. **LO 8/2021 Art. 26** — violencia vicaria as named category brings PA-pattern conduct into the family-violence frame directly
2. **CC Art. 158** — broad enforcement authority; courts can impose penalties, modify residence, order therapy, anything necessary
3. **Tribunal Supremo jurisprudence** — supreme-court-level convergence on behavior-frame analysis
4. **ECHR integration** — Bondavalli, Improta, Solarino, Strand Lobben directly applicable

## Practical use

Sample Spanish motion language:

> *Conforme a la doctrina del Tribunal Supremo y a la regulación del artículo 26 de la Ley Orgánica 8/2021, las conductas documentadas de [parental] constituyen una forma de violencia vicaria que afecta al interés superior del menor. Conforme al artículo 158 del Código Civil, se solicita respetuosamente al Juzgado la adopción de las siguientes medidas: [astreinte / cambio de modalidad de visita / evaluación pericial psicológica]. El cumplimiento del régimen de visitas establecido constituye una obligación cuyo incumplimiento sistemático no puede quedar sin respuesta judicial efectiva, conforme también a la jurisprudencia del TEDH (Bondavalli c. Italia, Improta c. Italia).*

## How this interacts with the international convergence

The 2022-2024 alignment across European supreme courts:

| Year | Jurisdiction | Doctrine |
|---|---|---|
| 2017 | ECHR | Solarino — no rubber-stamping coached refusal |
| 2018 | Germany BGH | Source-of-wishes investigation duty |
| 2021 | Spain LO 8/2021 | Violencia vicaria named statutorily |
| 2022 | Italy Cassazione | Behaviors not syndrome |
| 2023 | France Cass civ | Source-of-refusal investigation duty |
| 2023 | UK Re C | Behavior-frame operationalized |

Spanish PA cases benefit from citing the full European stack — all reach compatible conclusions.
