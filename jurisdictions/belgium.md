# Belgium

**Jurisdiction code:** `BE` · **Legal system:** civil-law  
**Language(s):** nl, fr, de  

Belgium is a federal civil-law jurisdiction structurally shaped by its community-linguistic division (Vlaamse Gemeenschap / Communauté française / Deutschsprachige Gemeinschaft). The federal Code civil (art. 374-374ter parental authority + droit aux relations personnelles), the Loi du 30 juillet 2013 (Tribunal de la famille et de la jeunesse / familierechter) and Code judiciaire constitute the substantive frame. The Cour de cassation / Hof van Cassatie has issued no apex ruling qualifying or disqualifying the PA/PAS construct. The principal institutional positional anchor is the IEFH/IGVM Recommandation 2023/001 — a structurally unique single federal bilingual (NL+FR) gender-equality regulator that explicitly warns the 'syndrome d'aliénation parentale' cannot be used to minimise or ignore (ex-)partner violence. Neither the Commission des psychologues / Psychologencommissie nor the BFP-BPF has issued a public PA position. Cross-border NL+BE Nederlandstalige and FR+BE francophone knowledge-spaces are structurally relevant.

## PA recognition status

- **Statutory:** silent
- **Apex court position:** no-apex-position
- **Professional regulator position:** silent

## Statutory framework

- **Code civil art. 374-374ter** — Code civil — autorité parentale et droit aux relations personnelles (1995) — <https://www.ejustice.just.fgov.be/cgi_loi/loi.pl>
  - Federal substantive frame for parental authority (art. 374 — exercice conjoint principe) and right of personal relations (art. 374bis — droit de l'enfant aux relations personnelles avec ses ascendants et grands-parents). The substantive Belgian hook for residence (hébergement) and contact disputes at Tribunal de la famille.
- **Code civil art. 387ter** — Code civil — inexécution des décisions relatives à l'hébergement (2007) — <https://www.ejustice.just.fgov.be/cgi_loi/loi.pl>
  - Provides remedies (astreinte, désignation provisoire d'un tiers, droit aux relations modifiées) where a parent refuses to execute a custody/contact order. The provision directly cited in Cass. 6 mars 2025 C.24.0157.F (the most recent Cour de cassation arrêt addressing PA-adjacent contact-frustration cases without addressing the construct's scientific validity).
- **Loi du 30 juillet 2013 portant création d'un Tribunal de la famille et de la jeunesse** — Tribunal de la famille / familierechtbank — specialist family-court forum (2013) — <https://www.ejustice.just.fgov.be/cgi_loi/change_lg.pl?language=fr&la=F&cn=2013073018&table_name=loi>
  - Constitutes the unified specialist family court at first instance with statutory jurisdiction over divorce, parental authority, residence, contact, alimony and DV protective-order proceedings. The Belgian counterpart to the Dutch Rechtbank familiekamer + Italian Tribunale per i minorenni / sezione famiglia. PA-adjacent fact-patterns are litigated at this forum at first instance.
- **Code judiciaire art. 1004/1 et suiv.** — Code judiciaire — désignation des experts judiciaires (CTU equivalent) (2007) — <https://www.ejustice.just.fgov.be/cgi_loi/loi.pl>
  - Procedural rules for designation and conduct of court-appointed experts including psychologists and psychiatrists in family-law proceedings. The Belgian counterpart to the Italian CTU regime and the Dutch RvdK / external expert mechanism.
- **Loi du 8 novembre 1993 protégeant le titre de psychologue** — Loi du 8 novembre 1993 — title-protection psychologue / titelbescherming psycholoog (1993) — <https://www.compsy.be/>
  - Federal title-protection law administered by the Commission des psychologues / Psychologencommissie. Establishes statutory title protection (without statutory deontological tuchtrecht beyond the Code de déontologie 2014 / Deontologische code). Has issued no PA-specific position. Title-protection silence on the PA construct is part of the broader Belgian double-silence pattern.
- **Loi du 4 avril 2014 — exercice de la psychothérapie** — Loi-cadre exercice de la psychothérapie (art. 68/1 et suiv. Loi coordonnée 10.5.2015 relative à l'exercice des professions des soins de santé) (2014) — <https://www.health.belgium.be/fr/sante/sante-mentale>
  - Federal regulation of psychotherapy practice; together with the 8.11.1993 psychologue-title law constitutes the substantive Belgian regulatory architecture for clinical-psychology / psychotherapy services. SPF Santé publique (Ministère fédéral) carries the regulatory authority via the Commission des psychologues and visa-aanvragen for psychotherapy authorisation.
- **Décret du 18 janvier 2018 portant le Code de la prévention, de l'aide à la jeunesse et de la protection de la jeunesse (FWB)** — Code de l'aide à la jeunesse de la Communauté française (2018) — <https://www.aidealajeunesse.cfwb.be/>
  - Code substantif de la Fédération Wallonie-Bruxelles. Crée le SAJ (Service d'aide à la jeunesse) et le SPJ (Service de protection judiciaire), avec les équipes SOS Enfants pour la maltraitance infantile. Cadre administratif et judiciaire de la prise en charge des situations familiales conflictuelles côté francophone.
- **Decreet Integrale Jeugdhulp (Vlaamse Gemeenschap)** — Decreet Integrale Jeugdhulp — Opgroeien (vroeger Jongerenwelzijn) (2013) — <https://www.opgroeien.be/>
  - Vlaamse decretale jeugdhulp-architectuur (sinds april 2019 onder agentschap Opgroeien). Cadre administratif et judiciaire flamand. Centra Algemeen Welzijnswerk (CAW), Vertrouwenscentra Kindermishandeling (VK, zes vestigingen) en gespecialiseerde jeugdhulpaanbieders zijn de operationele lijnen waarop PA-adjacent praktijk wordt afgehandeld.

## Apex courts

### Cour de cassation de Belgique / Hof van Cassatie
<https://www.cass.be/>
- **Cass. 6 mars 2025, C.24.0157.F — addresses inexécution des décisions du tribunal de la famille relatives à l'hébergement under art. 387ter Code civil. NOT a PA-construct-qualifying apex decision; the Cour de cassation has not yet ruled on the scientific validity of the parental-alienation construct.** (2025) — middle
- **No apex Cour de cassation / Hof van Cassatie ruling has been issued qualifying or disqualifying the PA/PAS construct. This places Belgium with the Netherlands and Germany in the EU6 'no-apex-PA-position' cluster, distinct from Italy (Cass. 9691/2022) and Spain (STS 519/2017).** (2026) — middle

### Cour constitutionnelle / Grondwettelijk Hof
<https://www.const-court.be/>
- **Federal constitutional court; no PA-specific ruling. The Court has addressed family-law statutory provisions in equality and child-welfare contexts but has not engaged the parental-alienation construct directly.** (2026) — middle

### Cours d'appel / Hoven van Beroep — family chambers
<https://www.rechtbanken-tribunaux.be/>
- **Cour d'appel Bruxelles, Liège, Mons, Anvers, Gand — family-appellate chambers review Tribunal de la famille decisions on hébergement and contact under Code civil art. 374-374ter. PA-adjacent fact-patterns are decided here under welfare/child-protection standards without direct PAS-construct adjudication.** (2026) — middle

## Professional regulators

- **Commission des psychologues / Psychologencommissie** — Federal title-protection commission for psychologists (Loi du 8 novembre 1993). Has issued NO public PA/PAS position statement. The Commission silence on the parental-alienation construct is structurally identical to the NIP / NVvP silence pattern in the Netherlands. — <https://www.compsy.be/>
- **Belgische Federatie van Psychologen / Fédération Belge des Psychologues (BFP-BPF)** — Federal umbrella professional association of psychologists (including VVKP — Vlaamse Vereniging van Klinisch Psychologen — and UPPCF — Union Professionnelle des Psychologues Cliniciens Francophones). BFP-BPF has issued no public PA/PAS recognition or critique statement. Federation silence is the second pillar of the Belgian psychology-association silence pattern. — <https://www.bfp-fbp.be/>
- **Hoge Gezondheidsraad / Conseil Supérieur de la Santé (CSS-HGR)** — Federal scientific health-policy advisory body. Has issued no parental-alienation-specific opinion. Absence of CSS-HGR engagement with the construct is consistent with the broader Belgian federal-institutional pattern of routing PA-construct comment via gender-equality (IEFH/IGVM) rather than health-policy regulators. — <https://www.health.belgium.be/fr/conseil-superieur-de-la-sante>
- **Institut pour l'égalité des femmes et des hommes / Instituut voor de Gelijkheid van Vrouwen en Mannen (IEFH/IGVM)** — Federal bilingual (NL+FR) gender-equality regulator. Recommandation 2023/001 'sur la violence entre (ex-)partenaires dans le contexte des divorces et séparations familiales' explicitly warns that 'le syndrome d'aliénation parentale ne peut être utilisé pour minimiser la violence entre (ex-)partenaires' and identifies the construct as susceptible to instrumentalisation by perpetrators of violence to disqualify victims' rupture-of-contact and abuse allegations. This is a STRUCTURALLY UNIQUE EU6 position — a single federal regulator with bilingual mandate articulating a critique-camp institutional anchor — without direct equivalent in the DE / FR / ES / IT / NL professional-regulator landscape. (2023-01-01) — <https://igvm-iefh.belgium.be/>
- **Délégué général aux droits de l'enfant (DGDE) — Communauté française** — Independent commissioner for children's rights in the Fédération Wallonie-Bruxelles. Has engaged complex divorce / PA-adjacent fact-patterns through child-rights primacy framing. Subsidiary critique-camp institutional voice alongside IEFH/IGVM. — <https://www.dgde.cfwb.be/>
- **Kinderrechtencommissariaat (KRC) — Vlaamse Gemeenschap** — Vlaams independent children's rights ombudsman. No specific PA-rejecting standpunt but a general kindbelang-priority frame. Cote vlaams the debate is less polarised and more methodological than in the Communauté française. — <https://www.kinderrechtencommissariaat.be/>
- **Coordination des ONG pour les droits de l'enfant (CODE)** — Civil-society coordination for children's-rights NGOs in the Communauté française. October 2023 analyse 'L'aliénation parentale : un concept à risques pour les enfants et leurs droits' is a load-bearing francophone Belgian critique anchor. — <https://www.lacode.be/>

## Anonymisation convention

Belgian Cour de cassation and Cours d'appel anonymise minor children consistently; adult parties in family-law published arrêts are typically referenced by initial (e.g., M. X / Mme Y) or generic descriptor. Ejustice.just.fgov.be publishes consolidated case law with reporting restrictions on minor identification. The Loi du 8 avril 1965 sur la protection de la jeunesse imposes confidentiality on jeugdrechtbank / tribunal de la jeunesse proceedings.

## Structural findings

- STRUCTURALLY UNIQUE EU6 SIGNATURE: The critique-camp institutional anchor lives at the FEDERAL GENDER-EQUALITY REGULATOR (IEFH/IGVM Recommandation 2023/001) — NOT at the psychology regulator. Belgium is the only EU6 jurisdiction where the critique-camp position is articulated at federal-regulator level rather than only at professional-association or judicial level. The IEFH/IGVM is uniquely positioned as a single federal bilingual (NL+FR) regulator covering both linguistic communities; this combined coverage gives the Recommandation 2023/001 a structural reach unavailable elsewhere in EU6.
- DOUBLE PROFESSIONAL-ASSOCIATION SILENCE: Neither the Commission des psychologues / Psychologencommissie nor the BFP-BPF has issued any public PA/PAS position. The Belgian psychology-profession silence is structurally identical to the NIP / NVvP silence pattern (Netherlands) but Belgium adds the critique-camp anchor at the gender-equality regulator that the Netherlands lacks. Belgian institutional positional weight therefore lives at FEDERAL GENDER-EQUALITY level plus CIVIL-SOCIETY level (CODE octobre 2023; Ligue des familles juin 2024; CERE asbl 2021), bypassing the silent psychology profession.
- LINGUISTIC-COMMUNITY ASYMMETRY (moderate): The critique-camp debate is more institutionally embedded in the Communauté française (CODE, Université des Femmes, Vie Féminine, Solidarité Femmes La Louvière, CVFE, Ligue des familles, CERE asbl) than in the Vlaamse Gemeenschap. Côté Vlaams, the debate is less polarised and more methodological: Kinderrechtencommissariaat has no PA-rejecting standpunt but a general kindbelang-priority frame; the Vlaamse forensic-clinical field leans on systemic methodologies (Lorentzhuis import). No Vlaams equivalent of the francophone 'contrôle coercitif' discourse exists.
- CROSS-BORDER NL-BE LORENTZHUIS AXIS is structural: the van Lawick + Visser Kinderen uit de Knel / No Kids in the Middle methodology is broadly adopted in Vlaamse jeugdhulp and CAW networks. Belgian middle-camp practitioners systematically cite Lorentzhuis over Gardnerian PAS terminology. Cross-link practitioner:nl.van-lawick-justine applies to multiple Belgian entries.
- CROSS-BORDER FR-BE CONTRÔLE COERCITIF AXIS: francophone Belgian critique references increasingly mobilise the contrôle coercitif frame (cf. Andreea Gruev-Vintila in France) to re-read situations qualified as PA as post-separation extensions of intimate-partner violence. Cross-link practitioner:fr.gruev-vintila-andreea and RTBF Investigation 2022 'mères protectrices diabolisées' apply to the francophone Belgian critique-camp sub-corpus.
- NO APEX COUR DE CASSATION / HOF VAN CASSATIE RULING ON THE PA CONSTRUCT: Cass. 6 mars 2025 C.24.0157.F is the most recent Cour de cassation arrêt addressing PA-adjacent contact-frustration cases but does not engage the construct's scientific validity. Belgium clusters with the Netherlands and Germany in the EU6 'no-apex-PA-position' bloc, distinct from Italy (Cass. 9691/2022) and Spain (STS 519/2017).
- INTERNAL POLARISATION: ACALPA (Association contre l'aliénation parentale, fondée 2005, recognition-camp) and La Mouette Belgique (recognition-camp, conférences Van Dieren / Van Gijseghem 2002-2014) constitute the francophone advocacy-recognition pole; opposite, Université des Femmes, SOS Inceste Belgique, Ligue des familles, Solidarité Femmes and CVFE constitute the critique pole. Middle-stance methodological position carried by yapaka.be authors (Yves-Hiram Haesevoets, Frédérique Van Leuven, Reine Vander Linden) and by Olivier Limet (sociologue, formateur, FR+BE).
- UNIVERSITY ANCHORS: ULB (Yves-Hiram Haesevoets, psychologie clinique; Christine Frisch-Desmarez, pédopsychiatrie/psychanalyse; Université des Femmes adjoint), UCLouvain (Jean-Louis Renchon, droit familial émérite, Centre de droit de la personne et de la famille; Pascale Jamoulle, anthropologie LAAP/IACCHOS), UMons (Pascale Jamoulle cross-appointment), Université de Namur + UHasselt (IEFH 2022 commissioned study on the role of juges de la famille in (ex-)partner-violence context), KU Leuven (Liesbet Stevens, droit pénal sexuel + adjunct-directeur IGVM-IEFH). No large recognition-camp Belgian university pole equivalent to the PASG anglo-saxon.
