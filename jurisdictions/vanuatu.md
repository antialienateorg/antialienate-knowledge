# Vanuatu (Republic of Vanuatu / Ripablik Blong Vanuatu)

**Jurisdiction code:** `VU` · **Legal system:** mixed  
**Language(s):** bi, en, fr  

Vanuatu is a Pacific Island mixed-legal-system republic combining English common-law substantive heritage (via Anglo-French Condominium 1906-1980 hybrid inheritance) with French civil-law substantive influence and constitutionally-recognised kastom (customary-law) personal-status jurisdiction operating through Island Courts. The hybrid Anglo-French legal-system inheritance is structurally distinctive globally — Vanuatu is the only state in the corpus with explicit Anglo-French dual-colonial-inheritance heritage. Family-law framework operates under the Control of Marriage Act, the Matrimonial Causes Act, the Maintenance of Children Act, the Family Protection Act 2008, and customary-law applied via Island Courts. Parental responsibility and child custody are governed by case-law applying the welfare-of-the-child principle. The Court of Appeal of Vanuatu is the apex court for civil and criminal matters. Family-law matters are heard at first instance in the Supreme Court (Family Division), Magistrates' Courts, and Island Courts for kastom matters. Psychology profession is regulated through the Ministry of Health framework. Vanuatu is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label; courts operate substantively under the welfare-of-the-child principle. Vanuatu is non-Hague Convention.

## Key developments

- **1980** — Vanuatu achieved independence from Anglo-French Condominium — retained both English common-law and French civil-law substantive influences.
- **1983** — Federal statute establishing Island Courts for kastom jurisdiction.
- **2008** — Federal statute on family protection orders enacted.
