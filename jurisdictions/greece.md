# Greece (Ελλάδα)

**Jurisdiction code:** `GR` · **Legal system:** civil-law  
**Language(s):** el  

Greece is a civil-law unitary republic whose family-court framework operates under the Astikos Kodikas (Civil Code) Book Four (Family Law). Parental responsibility (γονική μέριμνα / goniki merimna) is governed by AK arts. 1510-1541; joint exercise of parental responsibility was strengthened by Law 4800/2021 in force 16 September 2021, which established explicit statutory provisions on shared residence, contact-time presumptions, and contact-obstruction sanctions. The Areios Pagos (Court of Cassation, Athens) is the apex court for civil matters; family-law decisions reach Areios Pagos via the Efeteio (Court of Appeal) following first-instance Monomeles Protodikeio (District Court) determinations. Psychology profession is regulated under Law 991/1979 and the subsequent legal framework; statutory licensing operates under the Ministry of Health. Greece is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label, but the Law 4800/2021 explanatory memorandum and parliamentary debate engaged the PA-construct as part of its policy basis, making Greece notable within the corpus for explicit legislative reference to alienating-conduct concerns even without statutory PAS codification.

## Anonymisation convention

Greek family-law decisions are anonymised per Areios Pagos and Efeteio practice using initials. Published decisions on the Areios Pagos portal strip identifying details.

## Key developments

- **1979** — Federal statutory regulation of psychology profession enacted.
- **1983** — Substantial modernisation of Astikos Kodikas Book Four — abolition of patriarchal family-law structure; gender-equal parental responsibility; civil marriage; no-fault divorce.
- **2021** — Substantial reform of parental-responsibility framework in force 16 September 2021. Strengthened joint-exercise default; introduced presumption of minimum 1/3 contact-time; established statutory grounds for sanctioning sustained contact-obstruction. Legislative-record engagement with PA-construct reasoning in the explanatory memorandum and parliamentary debate.

## Structural findings

- Greece sits structurally within the EU civil-law cluster — Astikos Kodikas + Law 4800/2021 reform + Areios Pagos cassation apex. Law 4800/2021 makes Greece notable for explicit legislative-record engagement with PA-construct reasoning even without statutory PAS codification — structurally distinct from Brazil (Lei 12.318/2010) and Mexico (state codes) which codified PA-related provisions explicitly.
- Law 4800/2021 contact-time presumption (minimum 1/3) and obstruction-sanction framework places Greece structurally adjacent to Belgium's astreintes regime (Civil Code art. 387ter) and to the Brazilian Lei 12.318/2010 framework — without naming PA in statutory text.
