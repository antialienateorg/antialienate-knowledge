# Palau (Republic of Palau / Beluu er a Belau)

**Jurisdiction code:** `PW` · **Legal system:** common-law  
**Language(s):** en, pau  

Palau is a Pacific Island common-law republic in Compact of Free Association with the United States (Compact 1994). Family-law framework operates under the Palau National Code Title 21 (Domestic Relations) drawing on US-derivative common-law substantive heritage via Compact framework and prior US Trust Territory administration. Parental responsibility and child custody are governed by National Code Title 21 Chapters 1-7. The Supreme Court of Palau is the apex court for civil and criminal matters. Customary-law jurisdiction operates in parallel — Palau is structurally distinctive globally as the only state with constitutionally-recognised matrilineal customary-law framework (Belauan tradition). The Court of Common Pleas operates first-instance customary-law jurisdiction. Family-law matters are heard at first instance in the Supreme Court (Trial Division) and Court of Common Pleas. Psychology profession is regulated through the Ministry of Health framework. Palau is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label; courts operate substantively under the welfare-of-the-child principle. Palau is non-Hague Convention.
