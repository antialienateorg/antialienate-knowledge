# Lesotho (Kingdom of Lesotho / Muso oa Lesotho)

**Jurisdiction code:** `LS` · **Legal system:** mixed  
**Language(s):** en, st  

Lesotho is a Southern African mixed-legal-system constitutional monarchy combining Roman-Dutch civil-law substantive heritage (via Cape Colony) with English common-law procedural inheritance and customary-law personal-status jurisdiction (Sesotho customary law). Family-law framework operates under the Marriage Act 10/1974, Children's Protection and Welfare Act 7/2011, and Laws of Lerotholi (codified Sesotho customary law). Parental rights and child custody are governed by Children's Protection and Welfare Act Part IV. The Court of Appeal of Lesotho is the apex court for civil and criminal matters; the Constitutional Court (sitting as the High Court) operates constitutional review. Family-law matters are heard at first instance in the High Court (Family Division), Magistrates' Courts, and customary Local Courts. Psychology profession is regulated through the Lesotho Allied Health Professions Council under the Allied Health Professions Order. Lesotho is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label; courts operate substantively under the best-interests-of-the-child principle codified in Children's Protection and Welfare Act s. 4. Lesotho is non-Hague Convention.
