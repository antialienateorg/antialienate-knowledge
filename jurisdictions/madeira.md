# Madeira (Região Autónoma da Madeira / Autonomous Region of Madeira)

**Jurisdiction code:** `PT-MAD` · **Legal system:** civil-law  
**Language(s):** pt  

Madeira (Região Autónoma da Madeira / RAM) is a North-eastern Atlantic civil-law autonomous region of the Portuguese Republic comprising the Madeira archipelago (Madeira Island + Porto Santo + Desertas + Selvagens) located ~1,000 km southwest of mainland Portugal and ~700 km west of Morocco — structurally distinctive globally as one of only two Portuguese autonomous regions (with the Azores) constitutionally established under Portuguese Constitution 1976 Article 6 (modified subsequently), as one of nine EU 'outermost regions' (Régions ultrapériphériques / RUP) operating under EU Treaty Article 349 special-regime framework, and as the central jurisdiction of the Estatuto Político-Administrativo da Região Autónoma da Madeira 1991 (modernised 2006) which establishes Madeira's distinctive legislative-assembly framework with Lei Orgânica autonomy. Madeira has the constitutional power to enact regional legislative acts (decretos legislativos regionais) within its statutorily defined competence including specific powers over family-law-adjacent matters such as guardianship and child welfare. Family-law framework operates under the Portuguese Civil Code (Código Civil 1966, as amended) applied via Madeira regional administrative framework with Madeira-specific regulatory decrees in some areas. Parental authority (responsabilidade parental) and child custody operate under Portuguese Civil Code Articles 1877-1920 as substantially amended by Lei 61/2008 (Joint Parental Responsibility Reform). The Tribunal da Relação de Lisboa has jurisdiction over Madeira appellate matters (the Madeira Judicial District 'Comarca da Madeira' is integrated into the Lisbon Court of Appeals system); final appellate jurisdiction lies with the Portuguese Supreme Court of Justice (Supremo Tribunal de Justiça). Madeira is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label. Portugal is a Hague Convention 1980 party (acceded 6 December 1983) — Madeira Hague applicability via Portuguese territorial extension.

## Key developments

- **1419** — Portuguese discovery of Madeira by João Gonçalves Zarco and Tristão Vaz Teixeira in 1419 — beginning of Portuguese sovereignty over Madeira.
- **1976** — Portuguese Constitution of 2 April 1976 Article 6 establishing the autonomous regions of Madeira and Azores within the Portuguese Republic.
- **1991** — Portuguese Organic Law 13/91 of 5 June 1991 establishing Madeira's distinctive legislative-assembly framework — first comprehensive Madeira Political-Administrative Statute.
- **1997** — Amsterdam Treaty of 2 October 1997 establishing EU 'outermost regions' (RUP) special-regime framework — Madeira designated as RUP.
- **2006** — Lei Orgânica 1/2006 of 13 February 2006 comprehensively modernising Madeira Political-Administrative Statute.
- **2008** — Portuguese Law 61/2008 of 31 October 2008 substantially amending Portuguese Civil Code Articles 1877-1920 — introducing presumption of joint parental responsibility.
- **2009** — Lisbon Treaty effective 1 December 2009 continuing EU 'outermost regions' (RUP) framework via Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union Article 349.
