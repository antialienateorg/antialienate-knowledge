# Paracel Islands (西沙群島 / Quần đảo Hoàng Sa)

**Jurisdiction code:** `XX-PAR` · **Legal system:** civil-law  
**Language(s):** zh, vi  

The Paracel Islands (西沙群島 Xīshā Qúndǎo / Quần đảo Hoàng Sa) are a South China Sea disputed archipelago of ~30 small islands, cays, and reefs covering ~7,750 km² of maritime area — structurally distinctive globally as the only territorial dispute where a single state (People's Republic of China) gained de-facto control through a brief naval engagement (Battle of the Paracel Islands, 19 January 1974) against another state (Republic of Vietnam / South Vietnam) immediately prior to that other state's dissolution (30 April 1975). The PRC has administered the Paracels since 1974 and incorporated them into Sansha City (三沙市) of Hainan Province (since 2012) and subsequently Xisha District (西沙区) of Sansha City (since 2020). The Paracels are also claimed by Vietnam (as Hoàng Sa District of Đà Nẵng Municipality) and Taiwan (Republic of China). Unlike the more-multi-state-claimed Spratly Islands, the Paracels are a three-state-claim dispute (PRC, Vietnam, Taiwan), with only PRC maintaining physical occupation. The Paracels host the largest PRC South China Sea military deployment outside the Spratly artificial-island construction including Woody Island (永興島 / Yǒngxīng Dǎo / Đảo Phú Lâm), the administrative capital of Sansha City and PRC's largest South China Sea civilian-military settlement with ~1,000 permanent residents and runway-equipped military airbase. Family-law framework operates under the Chinese Civil Code (民法典 2020) and Chinese Marriage Law (婚姻法 1981, replaced by Civil Code Book V 2021), applied via Hainan Province / Sansha City administrative framework — Sansha City's Xisha District administrative seat is at Woody Island. Parental authority and child custody operate under Chinese Civil Code Book V Article 1058-1090 (婚姻家庭编). The Higher People's Court of Hainan Province is the apex regional court; final appellate jurisdiction lies with the Supreme People's Court of the People's Republic of China. The Paracels are silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label. Chinese mainland non-Hague-Convention-1980 status applies — Hague applies only via Hong Kong (1997 succession) and Macau (1999 succession) SAR-extension frameworks.

## Key developments

- **1932** — France formally claimed Paracel Islands on 13 April 1932 as part of French Indochina — beginning of modern multi-state-claim era.
- **1974** — PRC vs Republic of Vietnam naval engagement at Paracel Islands 19 January 1974 — PRC gained de-facto control of the entire Paracel archipelago against South Vietnam (which dissolved 30 April 1975).
- **2012** — PRC State Council established Sansha City of Hainan Province on 21 June 2012 — administrative seat at Woody Island in Paracels.
- **2020** — PRC State Council established Xisha District of Sansha City on 18 April 2020 — administrative framework for PRC-administered Paracels.
