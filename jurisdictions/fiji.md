# Fiji (Republic of Fiji)

**Jurisdiction code:** `FJ` · **Legal system:** common-law  
**Language(s):** en, fj  

Fiji is a Pacific Island common-law republic whose family-law framework operates under the Family Law Act 2003 (Act 18 of 2003, effective 1 November 2005) — comprehensive Australian-influenced family-law statute replacing prior Matrimonial Causes Act and Maintenance and Affiliation Act. Parental responsibility and child custody are governed by Family Law Act Part VI. The Supreme Court of Fiji is the apex court for civil and criminal matters. Family-law matters are heard at first instance in the Family Division of the High Court and Family Magistrates' Courts. Psychology profession is regulated through the Fiji Psychology Council under the Allied Health Practitioners Act. Fiji is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label; courts operate substantively under the welfare-of-the-child principle codified in Family Law Act s. 4. Fiji is non-Hague Convention.
