# Cyprus (Κύπρος / Kıbrıs)

**Jurisdiction code:** `CY` · **Legal system:** mixed  
**Language(s):** el, tr  

Cyprus is a Mediterranean unitary republic operating a mixed-legal-system framework combining common-law procedural inheritance from the British colonial period with civil-law statutory layers and the family-court jurisdictions for the recognised religious communities. Family-law matters are heard by specialised Family Courts (Οικογενειακά Δικαστήρια) under the Family Courts Law of 1990. Parental responsibility (γονική μέριμνα) is governed by the Children Law Cap. 352 as amended. The Supreme Court of Cyprus (Ανώτατο Δικαστήριο) operates as the apex court for civil and criminal matters and as the Constitutional Court. Psychology profession is regulated under the Psychologists Registration Law of 1995 (as amended) operated by the Cyprus Psychologists' Board (Συμβούλιο Εγγραφής Ψυχολόγων). Cyprus is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label; courts operate substantively under the welfare-of-the-child standard. Cyprus is a Council of Europe member; ECHR Strasbourg jurisprudence applies.

## Statutory framework

- **Children Law Cap. 352 (as amended)** — Children Law — Parental responsibility (1956) — <http://www.cylaw.com/>
  - Federal statute governing parental responsibility, custody and contact. Substantially amended over time including the framework for the welfare-of-the-child standard.
- **Family Courts Law 23/1990** — Family Courts Law 1990 (1990) — <http://www.cylaw.com/>
  - Federal statute establishing specialised Family Courts (Οικογενειακά Δικαστήρια) with jurisdiction over family-law matters.
- **Psychologists Registration Law 68(I)/1995** — Psychologists Registration Law 1995 (1995) — <http://www.cylaw.com/>
  - Federal statute regulating the psychology profession. Establishes the Cyprus Psychologists' Board (Συμβούλιο Εγγραφής Ψυχολόγων) and statutory registration. Statutory anchor for evaluator-quality in court-appointed psychological assessments.
