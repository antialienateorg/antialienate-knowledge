# Bolivia (Plurinational State of Bolivia / Estado Plurinacional de Bolivia)

**Jurisdiction code:** `BO` · **Legal system:** mixed  
**Language(s):** es, qu, ay  

Bolivia is an Andean South American mixed-legal-system plurinational state combining Spanish civil-law substantive heritage with constitutionally-recognised indigenous jurisdictional pluralism (Jurisdicción Indígena Originario Campesina). Family-law framework operates under the Family and Family-Procedural Code 2014 (Código de las Familias y del Proceso Familiar, Law 603 of 2014) replacing the 1972 Family Code. Parental authority and child custody are governed by Family Code arts. 35-49. The Supreme Court of Justice (Tribunal Supremo de Justicia) is the apex court for civil and criminal matters; the Plurinational Constitutional Tribunal (Tribunal Constitucional Plurinacional) operates separate constitutional review. The Constitution recognises Indigenous Originary Peasant Jurisdiction (JIOC) as a parallel jurisdiction. Family-law matters are heard at first instance in the Family Courts (Juzgados de Familia). Psychology profession is regulated through the Colegio de Psicólogos de Bolivia. Bolivia is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label; courts operate substantively under the superior-interest-of-the-child standard codified in Niñez y Adolescencia Code 2014 art. 12. Bolivia is non-Hague Convention.
