# Mauritania (Islamic Republic of Mauritania / الجمهورية الإسلامية الموريتانية)

**Jurisdiction code:** `MR` · **Legal system:** religious-law  
**Language(s):** ar  

Mauritania is a West African religious-law Islamic republic operating under a Maliki-Sunni Sharia framework. Family-law framework operates under the Personal Status Code 2001 (Law 2001-052, Code du Statut Personnel) drawing on Maliki jurisprudence with codification. Mauritania is structurally distinctive within the corpus as the only Islamic Republic in West Africa and within the Sahel cluster — Constitution 1991 (revised 2017) establishes Islam as state religion and Sharia as the source of law. Custody (hadana) and guardianship (wilaya) are governed by Personal Status Code arts. 124-145. The Supreme Court (Cour Suprême) is the apex court for civil and criminal matters; the Constitutional Council (Conseil Constitutionnel) operates separate constitutional review. Family-law matters are heard at first instance in the Court of First Instance with Personal Status section. Psychology profession is regulated through the Ministry of Health framework. Mauritania is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label; courts operate substantively under the welfare-of-the-child standard. Mauritania is non-Hague Convention.
