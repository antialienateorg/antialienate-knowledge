# Sardinia (Sardegna / Sardigna)

**Jurisdiction code:** `IT-SRD` · **Legal system:** civil-law  
**Language(s):** it, sc, ca, sdc, sdn  

Sardinia (Sardegna in Italian / Sardigna in Sardinian / officially Regione Autonoma della Sardegna) is a Mediterranean civil-law autonomous region of the Italian Republic comprising the island of Sardinia (second-largest island in the Mediterranean, ~24,090 km²) and surrounding minor archipelagos (La Maddalena, Asinara, San Pietro, Sant'Antioco) — structurally distinctive globally as the only Italian autonomous region operating a constitutionally-protected multi-linguistic minority framework with Sardinian (Sardu), Algherese Catalan (Alguerès), Sassarese (Sassarese), Gallurese (Gadduresu), Tabarchino, and Maddalenino as recognised minority languages under Italian Law 482/1999 and Sardinia's Sardinian Linguistic Policy Law 22/2018, as the second Italian autonomous region established (Statuto Speciale 26 February 1948, ~9 months after Sicily but ~2 months before formal Italian Constitution), and as the central jurisdiction of Italy's longest-tradition continuous customary-law-influenced civil-law framework dating from the medieval Carta de Logu (~14th-century Sardinian customary-law code by Eleonora of Arborea, 1392 Logudoro version). Sardinia's autonomy framework was established by Constitutional Law 3/1948 of 26 February 1948 (Statuto Speciale della Regione Sardegna). The Statuto Speciale grants Sardinia exclusive legislative competence over an extensive range of matters including agriculture, fisheries, industry, mining, and local administration. Family-law framework operates under the Italian Civil Code (Codice Civile 1942, as amended by Law 54/2006 Joint Custody Reform) applied via Sardinian autonomous-region administrative framework with Italian Law 482/1999 linguistic-minority protections. Parental authority (responsabilità genitoriale) and child custody operate under Italian Civil Code Articles 315-342-bis as amended. The Court of Appeal of Cagliari (or Sassari) is the apex regional appellate court for Sardinian civil and criminal matters; final appellate jurisdiction lies with the Italian Court of Cassation and the Italian Constitutional Court. Sardinia is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label. Italy is a Hague Convention 1980 party (acceded 22 February 1985) — Sardinia Hague applicability via Italian territorial extension.

## Statutory framework

- **Statuto Speciale della Regione Sardegna 1948 (Constitutional Law 3/1948)** — Sardinian Special Statute (1948) — <https://www.regione.sardegna.it/>
  - Italian Constitutional Law of 26 February 1948 establishing the Sardinian autonomous-region framework — second Italian autonomous region (after Sicily 1946).
- **Italian Law 482/1999 (Linguistic Minorities Protection)** — Italian Linguistic Minorities Protection Law (1999) — <https://www.normattiva.it/>
  - Italian Law of 15 December 1999 establishing protection framework for historical linguistic minorities — recognises Sardinian, Catalan (Alguerese), and other Sardinian minority languages.
- **Sardinian Linguistic Policy Law 22/2018** — Sardinian Linguistic Policy Law (2018) — <https://www.regione.sardegna.it/>
  - Sardinian Regional Law 22/2018 of 3 July 2018 establishing Sardinia's distinctive multi-linguistic-minority policy framework including Sardinian (Sardu), Algherese Catalan (Alguerès), Sassarese, Gallurese, Tabarchino, and Maddalenino.
- **Italian Codice Civile Articles 315-342-bis (as amended by Law 54/2006)** — Italian Civil Code — Parental Authority (2006) — <https://www.normattiva.it/>
  - Italian Civil Code articles governing responsabilità genitoriale and child custody applicable in Sardinia.
- **Carta de Logu (Sardinian Medieval Customary-Law Code)** — Carta de Logu (1392) — <https://www.regione.sardegna.it/>
  - Medieval Sardinian customary-law code by Eleonora of Arborea — 1392 Logudoro version. Foundational influence on Italy's longest-tradition continuous customary-law-influenced civil-law framework.
