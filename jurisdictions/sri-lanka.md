# Sri Lanka (ශ්‍රී ලංකා / இலங்கை)

**Jurisdiction code:** `LK` · **Legal system:** mixed  
**Language(s):** si, ta, en  

Sri Lanka is a South Asian mixed-legal-system unitary republic combining Roman-Dutch civil-law substantive heritage with English common-law procedural inheritance, plus religious-community personal laws (Kandyan Sinhalese, Thesawalamai Tamil, Muslim). Family law operates under the General Marriage Ordinance 1907 and Civil Procedure Code for general matters, with Kandyan Marriage and Divorce Act 1952, Thesawalamai (Jaffna Tamils), and Muslim Marriage and Divorce Act 1951 for community-specific matters. Custody is governed by general law via District Courts. The Supreme Court of Sri Lanka is the apex court for civil, criminal, constitutional and administrative matters. Psychology profession regulation is administered through the Sri Lanka Medical Council framework with the Sri Lanka Psychological Association operating professional standards. Sri Lanka is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label; courts operate substantively under the welfare-of-the-child standard. Sri Lanka acceded to the Hague Convention 1980 effective 1 August 2002.
