# Mount Athos (Autonomous Monastic State of the Holy Mountain / Αυτόνομη Μοναστική Πολιτεία Αγίου Όρους)

**Jurisdiction code:** `GR-ATH` · **Legal system:** mixed  
**Language(s):** el  

Mount Athos (Άγιον Όρος Ágion Óros 'Holy Mountain' / Αυτόνομη Μοναστική Πολιτεία Αγίου Όρους Autónomi Monastikí Politeía Agíou Órous 'Autonomous Monastic State of the Holy Mountain') is a Mediterranean mixed-system theocratic monastic autonomous state within the Hellenic Republic on the Athos peninsula of Chalkidiki, Central Macedonia — structurally distinctive globally as the only theocratic monastic republic operating within a European Union member state under EU Treaty special-status framework (Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union Declaration No. 4 attached to the Final Act of the Maastricht Treaty 1992, preserving Mount Athos's traditional status including the avaton (ἄβατον 'inaccessibility' / women-and-female-domestic-animals exclusion in effect since the Imperial decree of Constantine IX Monomachos in 1045 — making it the oldest continuously-operating ethnic-gender-exclusion framework in Europe, ~980 years), as one of only two state-level entities operating an internal-self-government-on-territory-of-EU-member-state framework alongside Madeira/Azores autonomy (but unique in its theocratic character), and as the spiritual centre of Orthodox Christian monasticism with 20 ruling monasteries and ~2,200 monks under canonical jurisdiction of the Ecumenical Patriarchate of Constantinople (despite physical location in Greece). Mount Athos has been a self-governing monastic community since 963 CE (founding of the Great Lavra by Athanasios the Athonite) and operates under the 1924 Constitutional Charter of the Holy Mountain (Καταστατικός Χάρτης τοῦ Ἁγίου Ὄρους). Civilian and family-law framework operates under the Hellenic Civil Code (Αστικός Κώδικας 1946) applied via Greek civil-administration overlay, but Mount Athos's permanent residents are exclusively monks subject to monastic canon law (Athonite Typikon framework). Civil-court matters from Mount Athos go to the Greek Court of Polygyros (Chalkidiki); final appellate jurisdiction lies with the Supreme Court of Greece (Areios Pagos). Mount Athos is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label — family-law framework is theoretical given exclusively-monk permanent population. Greece is a Hague Convention 1980 party (acceded 19 March 1993) — Mount Athos Hague applicability via Greek territorial extension is theoretical given exclusively-monk permanent population.
