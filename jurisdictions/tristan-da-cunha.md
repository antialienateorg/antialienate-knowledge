# Tristan da Cunha (Tristan da Cunha Group)

**Jurisdiction code:** `SH-TDC` · **Legal system:** common-law  
**Language(s):** en  

Tristan da Cunha is a South Atlantic common-law dependency of the British Overseas Territory of Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha — structurally distinctive globally as the most remote inhabited archipelago on Earth (nearest inhabited land is Saint Helena ~2,430 km north; nearest continental land is South Africa ~2,810 km east; nearest other major land is South America/Argentina ~3,360 km west), as the only state-level entity operating a complete-community-ownership land framework (all land on Tristan da Cunha is constitutionally communally owned via the Tristan da Cunha Land Tenure framework — no private land ownership permitted), and as the central jurisdiction of the 1961 Volcanic Eruption Evacuation framework (Tristan da Cunha's entire population of ~290 was evacuated to the UK following the 10 October 1961 volcanic eruption of Queen Mary's Peak — the only complete state-level civilian-population evacuation due to natural disaster in modern history; the population voted to return to Tristan da Cunha in November 1962 and was repatriated in November 1963). Tristan da Cunha comprises the main inhabited island of Tristan (~98 km²) plus the uninhabited Nightingale, Inaccessible, Gough, and Middle Islands. The permanent population is ~270 in the single settlement of Edinburgh of the Seven Seas, descended from a small founding population including a single 1816 British military garrison commander (Corporal William Glass) and subsequent ship-wrecked sailors and women from Saint Helena — making Tristan da Cunha distinctive globally as the only state-level entity with a population traceable to a single 1816 founding cohort. Family-law framework operates under the English law via the Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha Constitution Order 2009 with local Tristan da Cunha Council Ordinances. Parental authority and child custody operate under English law adapted to Tristan da Cunha circumstances. The Tristan da Cunha Magistrate's Court is the first-instance court; the Supreme Court of Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha is the intermediate appellate court; final appellate jurisdiction lies with the UK Privy Council (Judicial Committee). Tristan da Cunha is silent on 'parental alienation' as a statutory label. UK is a Hague Convention 1980 party (acceded 20 May 1986) — Tristan da Cunha Hague applicability via UK territorial extension.

## Key developments

- **1816** — British military annexation of Tristan da Cunha on 14 August 1816 — beginning of British sovereignty. Corporal William Glass remained on Tristan da Cunha after garrison withdrawal in 1817 — founding cohort of current population.
- **1961** — Volcanic eruption of Queen Mary's Peak on 10 October 1961 — Tristan da Cunha's entire population of ~290 was evacuated to UK. Only complete state-level civilian-population evacuation due to natural disaster in modern history.
- **1963** — Tristan da Cunha population voted to return in November 1962 and was repatriated in November 1963 — most remote permanent civilian community to undergo complete-population evacuation and return in modern history.
- **1986** — Hague Convention 1980 territorial extension by UK to Tristan da Cunha effective 1 August 1986.
- **2009** — UK Order in Council of 8 July 2009 establishing the consolidated constitutional framework for Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha.
